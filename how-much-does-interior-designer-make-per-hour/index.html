How Much Does An Interior Designer Make Per Hour

Written by Frank Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an interior designer, but wondering how much you'll make per hour? Or maybe you're already an interior designer and want to know if you're being paid fairly? Whatever the case may be, understanding the average hourly rate for interior designers can help you make informed decisions about your career.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for interior designers in the United States is $25.86 per hour. However, the hourly rate can vary depending on a variety of factors such as education, experience, location, and industry.

In addition, interior designers who own their own businesses or work as independent contractors may charge higher hourly rates than those who work for a company or corporation. It's important to keep these factors in mind when determining your own hourly rate as an interior designer.

Factors That Affect an Interior Designer's Hourly Rate

When determining an hourly rate, there are several factors to consider:

Education and Experience

The more education and experience an interior designer has, the higher their hourly rate is likely to be. Interior designers with a bachelor's or master's degree and several years of experience may be able to charge more per hour than those with only a high school diploma and limited experience.

Location

The location of an interior designer can also affect their hourly rate. Interior designers in larger cities or areas with a higher cost of living may be able to charge more per hour than those in smaller towns or areas with a lower cost of living.

Industry

The industry in which an interior designer works can also impact their hourly rate. For example, an interior designer who primarily works with high-end residential clients may be able to charge a higher hourly rate than one who primarily works with commercial clients.

Specialization

Interior designers who specialize in a specific area such as kitchen design or sustainable design may be able to charge a higher hourly rate than those who offer general interior design services.

Personal Experience

As an interior designer with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that hourly rates can vary widely depending on a variety of factors. When starting out in the industry, I charged around $20 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and built a strong portfolio, I was able to increase my hourly rate to $75 per hour. It's important to remember that your hourly rate will likely increase as you gain more experience and build a strong reputation in the industry.

How to Determine Your Hourly Rate

If you're an interior designer trying to determine your hourly rate, there are several steps you can take:

Research

Research the average hourly rate for interior designers in your area and industry. This can give you a good starting point for determining your own rate.

Evaluate Your Experience and Education

Consider your education and experience when determining your hourly rate. If you have a master's degree and several years of experience, you may be able to charge more than someone with only a high school diploma and limited experience.

Determine Your Expenses

Calculate your expenses such as rent, utilities, and supplies. You'll need to make sure your hourly rate covers these expenses in addition to your salary.

Consider Your Profit Margin

Remember that you're running a business and need to make a profit. Consider your profit margin when determining your hourly rate.

Question and Answer

Q: Can interior designers earn more than the median hourly rate?

A: Yes, interior designers with more education, experience, and a strong reputation in the industry can earn more than the median hourly rate.

Q: What is the range of hourly rates for interior designers?

A: Hourly rates for interior designers can range from $15 per hour to $150 per hour depending on various factors.

Q: Can interior designers charge a flat fee instead of an hourly rate?

A: Yes, interior designers can charge a flat fee for their services depending on the project and client needs.

Q: Do interior designers get paid more if they work for a large corporation?

A: It depends on the corporation and the individual's experience and education. Interior designers who work for large corporations may be able to earn a higher hourly rate than those who work for smaller companies.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Interior Designer Make Per Hour

As an interior designer, your hourly rate can vary depending on a variety of factors such as education, experience, location, and industry. It's important to research the average hourly rate for interior designers in your area and industry, evaluate your own education and experience, determine your expenses, and consider your profit margin when determining your hourly rate. Remember that your hourly rate will likely increase as you gain more experience and build a strong reputation in the industry.
