Classic Italian Lasagna Recipe With Ricotta Cheese

Written by Daniel Jun 05, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking for a classic Italian dish that will take your taste buds on a journey to Italy, then look no further than lasagna. The combination of layers of pasta, rich tomato sauce, creamy ricotta cheese, and savory meat creates a mouth-watering experience that is hard to resist. However, making lasagna from scratch can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this classic Italian lasagna recipe with ricotta cheese.

Lasagna can be a tricky dish to make, especially if you're not familiar with Italian cooking. One of the biggest pain points is getting the proportions of the ingredients right. You don't want your lasagna to be too dry or too soupy. Another pain point is getting the layering right. Each layer needs to be just the right amount of thickness so that the flavors can meld together perfectly. Finally, getting the cooking time and temperature right can be a challenge. You want your lasagna to be cooked all the way through, but not burnt.

The target of this classic Italian lasagna recipe with ricotta cheese is to provide you with a step-by-step guide to making the perfect lasagna. We'll cover everything from the ingredients you'll need, to how to layer your lasagna, to how long to cook it for. By the end of this article, you'll have a delicious lasagna that will make your taste buds sing.

In summary, this article will provide you with a classic Italian lasagna recipe with ricotta cheese. We'll cover the pain points that come with making lasagna, and provide you with a step-by-step guide to making the perfect lasagna.

The Ingredients You'll Need

I remember the first time I made lasagna. I spent hours scouring the internet for the perfect recipe, only to find that most of them required a laundry list of ingredients that I didn't have on hand. That's why I love this classic Italian lasagna recipe with ricotta cheese. The ingredients are simple, and you likely have most of them in your pantry already. Here's what you'll need:

  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 jar of tomato sauce
  • 1 box of lasagna noodles (no-boil or regular)
  • 1 container of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of pepper

Now that you have your ingredients, let's get started on making the perfect lasagna.

How to Make Classic Italian Lasagna Recipe with Ricotta Cheese

The first step in making lasagna is to cook your ground beef. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat, and add your ground beef. Cook until the beef is browned, stirring occasionally. Drain any excess fat.

Next, add your jar of tomato sauce to the skillet with the ground beef. Stir until the sauce is evenly combined with the beef. Let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will allow the flavors to meld together.

While your beef and sauce are simmering, it's time to prepare your lasagna noodles. If you're using regular noodles, cook them according to the package instructions. If you're using no-boil noodles, you don't need to cook them ahead of time.

In a separate bowl, mix together your ricotta cheese, egg, salt, and pepper. This mixture will be used for the middle layer of your lasagna.

Now it's time to start layering your lasagna. Start by spreading a thin layer of your beef and tomato sauce mixture on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Next, add a layer of noodles. Spread a layer of your ricotta cheese mixture on top of the noodles. Add another layer of noodles, and then a layer of your beef and tomato sauce mixture. Top the beef mixture with a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese.

Repeat the layering process until you've used all of your ingredients. Make sure to end with a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese on top.

Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Cover your lasagna with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Tips for Making the Perfect Lasagna

Now that you know how to make lasagna, here are a few tips to ensure that your lasagna turns out perfectly:

  • Use a 9x13 inch baking dish to ensure that your lasagna layers evenly.
  • Don't overcook your noodles. If you're using regular noodles, cook them until they're al dente. If you're using no-boil noodles, they will cook in the oven.
  • Let your lasagna cool for a few minutes before cutting into it. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to cut.

FAQs

What can I substitute ricotta cheese with?

You can substitute ricotta cheese with cottage cheese or mascarpone cheese.

Can I use ground turkey instead of ground beef?

Yes, you can use ground turkey instead of ground beef. You can also use a combination of ground beef and ground pork.

Can I make lasagna ahead of time?

Yes, you can make lasagna ahead of time. You can assemble your lasagna, cover it with foil, and refrigerate it for up to 24 hours. When you're ready to bake it, remove the foil and bake it according to the recipe instructions.

Can I freeze lasagna?

Yes, you can freeze lasagna. Assemble your lasagna and cover it with foil. Place it in the freezer for up to 3 months. When you're ready to bake it, remove it from the freezer and let it thaw in the refrigerator overnight. Remove the foil and bake it according to the recipe instructions.

Conclusion of Classic Italian Lasagna Recipe with Ricotta Cheese

There you have it, a classic Italian lasagna recipe with ricotta cheese that will make your taste buds sing. By following this step-by-step guide, you'll have a delicious lasagna that you can enjoy with family and friends. Remember to use a 9x13 inch baking dish, not to overcook your noodles, and let your lasagna cool before cutting into it. Enjoy!
