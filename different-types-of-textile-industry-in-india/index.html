Different Types Of Textile Industry In India

Written by Eveline Jul 26, 2023 · 5 min read

India is known for its diverse textile industry that has been flourishing for centuries. The industry plays a significant role in the country's economy, providing employment opportunities to millions of people. However, with the growth of the industry, there are various pain points related to it.

From the lack of skilled labor to the high cost of raw materials, the textile industry in India faces many challenges. Moreover, the competition from other countries and the fast-changing fashion trends also make it difficult for the industry to keep up.

In this article, we will discuss different types of textile industry in India and how they contribute to the country's economy. We will also explore the challenges faced by the industry and how it can overcome them to stay competitive.

To summarize, in this article, we will cover the different types of textile industry in India, their contribution to the economy, the challenges faced by the industry, and how it can overcome them. We will also discuss the future of the industry and its potential for growth.

Handloom Industry

One of the oldest and traditional forms of textile industry in India is handloom. The handloom industry is spread across the country and is known for its unique and intricate designs. Handloom fabrics are made entirely by hand, from spinning the yarn to weaving the fabric.

I come from a small village in West Bengal, where the handloom industry is the main source of income for many families. Growing up, I saw my grandmother weaving beautiful sarees and dhotis on her handloom. The handloom industry not only supports the livelihood of many families but also preserves the rich cultural heritage of the country.

Handloom fabrics are eco-friendly, sustainable, and have a high demand in the global market. However, the industry faces many challenges, such as the lack of modern infrastructure, access to credit, and marketing facilities. The government has taken various initiatives to support the handloom industry, including providing financial assistance, setting up handloom clusters, and promoting handloom products through exhibitions and fairs.

Powerloom Industry

The powerloom industry is the largest segment of the textile industry in India, contributing to around 60% of the total cloth production. Powerloom fabrics are made using power-driven machines, which can produce fabrics at a faster rate than handlooms.

My uncle owns a powerloom factory in Surat, Gujarat, which is known as the textile hub of India. The powerloom industry has transformed the textile sector in Surat, providing employment opportunities to thousands of people. The industry has also attracted many entrepreneurs and investors, leading to the growth of ancillary industries such as dyeing, printing, and embroidery.

However, the powerloom industry also faces many challenges, such as the high cost of raw materials, competition from other countries, and the lack of skilled labor. The government has taken various initiatives to support the powerloom industry, such as providing subsidies for modernization, setting up textile parks, and promoting exports.

Handicraft Industry

The handicraft industry in India is known for its unique and artistic products made by skilled artisans. The handicraft industry includes various products such as carpets, rugs, durries, wall hangings, and home decor items.

My friend's mother runs a handicraft business in Jaipur, Rajasthan, known for its exquisite handicraft products. The handicraft industry not only provides employment opportunities to artisans but also promotes traditional art and culture.

However, the handicraft industry also faces many challenges, such as the lack of modern technology, access to credit, and marketing facilities. The government has taken various initiatives to support the handicraft industry, such as providing financial assistance, setting up artisan clusters, and promoting exports.

Textile Recycling Industry

The textile recycling industry in India is a relatively new but growing segment of the textile industry. The textile recycling industry collects, sorts, and processes used textiles to create new products such as yarn, fabric, and clothing.

My sister works for a textile recycling company in Bangalore, which collects used clothes from various sources such as households, retailers, and manufacturers. The textile recycling industry not only promotes sustainability but also provides employment opportunities to many people.

However, the textile recycling industry also faces many challenges, such as the lack of awareness among people, the high cost of technology, and the unorganized nature of the industry. The government has taken various initiatives to support the textile recycling industry, such as providing financial assistance and setting up recycling centers.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the future of the textile industry in India?

A: The textile industry in India has a bright future, with the growing demand for sustainable and eco-friendly products. The industry is also adopting modern technology and automation to stay competitive.

Q: What are the challenges faced by the textile industry in India?

A: The challenges faced by the textile industry in India include the lack of skilled labor, the high cost of raw materials, competition from other countries, and the fast-changing fashion trends.

Q: How is the government supporting the textile industry in India?

A: The government is supporting the textile industry in India by providing financial assistance, setting up textile parks and clusters, promoting exports, and introducing policies to promote sustainability and innovation.

Q: What is the contribution of the textile industry to the Indian economy?

A: The textile industry is one of the largest sectors of the Indian economy, contributing to around 7% of the GDP and providing employment opportunities to millions of people.

Conclusion of Different Types of Textile Industry in India

The textile industry in India is diverse and rich, with various segments such as handloom, powerloom, handicraft, and textile recycling. Each segment has its unique characteristics, challenges, and potential for growth. The government has taken various initiatives to support the industry, but there is still a long way to go to overcome the challenges and stay competitive in the global market. With the right policies, infrastructure, and innovation, the textile industry in India can continue to flourish and contribute to the country's economy.
