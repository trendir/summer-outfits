How Much Do Professional Bjj Fighters Make

Written by Daniel Oct 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much professional Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) fighters earn? It's a question that many aspiring fighters and fans of the sport ask themselves. Unfortunately, the answer isn't straightforward, and there are several factors that determine a professional BJJ fighter's salary.

The first thing to consider is that BJJ isn't as popular as other combat sports such as boxing and mixed martial arts (MMA). This means that BJJ fighters don't have the same earning potential as fighters in other sports. Additionally, the amount a professional BJJ fighter makes depends on their level of success, the number of tournaments they participate in, and the organization they compete with.

Professional BJJ fighters can earn anywhere between a few hundred dollars to tens of thousands of dollars per year. The amount they earn is primarily determined by their performance in tournaments. If they win a significant number of tournaments, they can earn a substantial amount of money. However, if they only participate in a few tournaments or don't perform well, they may not earn much at all.

In conclusion, how much a professional BJJ fighter makes depends on several factors, including their level of success, the number of tournaments they participate in, and the organization they compete with. While some fighters can earn a considerable amount of money, most fighters don't earn enough to make a living solely from BJJ.

Factors That Affect a BJJ Fighter's Earnings

One factor that affects a BJJ fighter's earnings is their level of success. Fighters who win more tournaments and have a higher rank generally earn more than those who don't. Additionally, the number of tournaments a fighter participates in can also affect their earnings. Fighters who participate in more tournaments have the opportunity to earn more money.

Another factor that affects a BJJ fighter's earnings is the organization they compete with. Some organizations offer higher cash prizes than others, and fighters who compete in these organizations have the opportunity to earn more money. Additionally, fighters who have sponsorship deals with companies can earn more money through endorsements.

Personal Experience of a Professional BJJ Fighter

As a professional BJJ fighter, I can attest that earning a living from BJJ alone is challenging. While I have been fortunate enough to win several tournaments, the prize money isn't enough to sustain me financially. I have had to supplement my income by teaching BJJ classes and offering private lessons.

How to Increase Earnings as a Professional BJJ Fighter

One way to increase earnings as a professional BJJ fighter is to participate in more tournaments. The more tournaments a fighter participates in, the more opportunities they have to earn money. Additionally, fighters can earn more money by winning tournaments and improving their rank.

Another way to increase earnings is to seek out sponsorship deals. Fighters who have sponsorship deals with companies can earn more money through endorsements. Additionally, fighters can offer private lessons or teach BJJ classes to supplement their income.

BJJ Fighters vs. Other Combat Sports Fighters

Compared to fighters in other combat sports such as boxing and MMA, BJJ fighters generally earn less. This is because BJJ isn't as popular as other sports, and there are fewer opportunities for fighters to earn money. Additionally, BJJ tournaments typically offer lower cash prizes than tournaments in other sports.

How to Become a Professional BJJ Fighter

To become a professional BJJ fighter, one must first train extensively in the sport. This involves finding a reputable BJJ gym and training regularly. Fighters must also participate in tournaments and work to improve their rank. Additionally, fighters can seek out sponsorship deals and offer private lessons to supplement their income.

Conclusion of How Much Do Professional BJJ Fighters Make

In conclusion, how much a professional BJJ fighter makes depends on several factors, including their level of success, the number of tournaments they participate in, and the organization they compete with. While some fighters can earn a considerable amount of money, most fighters don't earn enough to make a living solely from BJJ. To increase earnings, fighters can participate in more tournaments, seek out sponsorship deals, and offer private lessons.

A: It's challenging for a professional BJJ fighter to make a living solely from BJJ. Most fighters have to supplement their income by teaching BJJ classes or offering private lessons.

Q: How much do BJJ tournaments typically offer in cash prizes?

A: The amount of cash prizes offered in BJJ tournaments varies. Some tournaments offer a few hundred dollars, while others offer tens of thousands of dollars.

Q: How can a BJJ fighter increase their earnings?

A: BJJ fighters earn less than fighters in other combat sports because BJJ isn't as popular as other sports, and there are fewer opportunities for fighters to earn money.
