Popular Toy Stores In The 80S A Nostalgic Look Back

Written by Eveline Sep 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you remember the days when going to the toy store was the highlight of your week? When you could spend hours browsing the aisles, dreaming of the latest action figures, board games, and dolls? For many of us who grew up in the 80s, toy stores were a magical place where anything was possible. In this post, we'll take a trip down memory lane and explore some of the most popular toy stores of the era.

For parents in the 80s, toy stores could be a double-edged sword. On the one hand, they offered endless possibilities for keeping kids entertained and happy. On the other hand, they could be overwhelming and expensive. With so many options to choose from, it was easy to feel like you were never buying enough. And of course, there was always the risk of a temper tantrum if you didn't get the toy your child had their heart set on.

But for kids, toy stores were pure bliss. They were a place to let your imagination run wild and explore new worlds. Whether you were into action figures, dolls, or board games, there was always something new and exciting to discover.

In this post, we'll take a look at some of the most popular toy stores of the 80s, from the big chains to the mom-and-pop shops.

Toys R Us: The Ultimate Toy Store

For many of us, Toys R Us was the ultimate toy store. With its bright colors, towering shelves, and endless aisles of toys, it was a child's paradise. You could spend hours exploring the different sections, from the action figures to the board games to the bikes and scooters.

One of my favorite memories of Toys R Us was the video game section. I can still remember the rows of cartridges and CDs, each one promising a new world of adventure and excitement. And of course, there was always the temptation of the demo kiosks, where you could try out the latest games before you bought them.

While Toys R Us may be gone now, it will always hold a special place in the hearts of those who grew up in the 80s.

Kay-Bee Toys: The Neighborhood Toy Store

While Toys R Us was the big chain store, Kay-Bee Toys was the neighborhood toy store. With its smaller size and more intimate feel, it was the perfect place to find unique toys and games that you couldn't find anywhere else.

One of my favorite things about Kay-Bee Toys was the bargain bin. This was where you could find discounted toys from previous seasons, or toys that had been discontinued altogether. It was always exciting to see what treasures you could find in the bin, and it was a great way to stretch your allowance money.

Kay-Bee Toys may not have been as flashy as Toys R Us, but it had its own charm and appeal.

The History of Lionel Kiddie City

Lionel Kiddie City was a chain of toy stores that operated from 1962 to 1994. With its bright colors and playful atmosphere, it was a favorite among kids and parents alike.

One of the things that set Lionel Kiddie City apart from other toy stores was its focus on educational toys. While there were plenty of action figures and dolls to choose from, there were also science kits, puzzles, and other toys that encouraged learning and exploration.

Lionel Kiddie City may have been short-lived, but it left a lasting impression on those who shopped there.

How to Find Your Local Toy Store

If you're feeling nostalgic for the toy stores of the 80s, the good news is that there are still plenty of local toy stores around. These stores may not have the same selection as the big chains, but they often have a more personal touch and a focus on unique and handmade toys.

To find your local toy store, try doing a search online for "local toy stores" or "indie toy stores". You can also check local directories or ask friends and family for recommendations.

Conclusion of Popular Toy Stores in the 80s

The toy stores of the 80s may be a thing of the past, but their memory lives on. Whether you were a Toys R Us kid or a Kay-Bee Toys fan, these stores were a place of wonder and excitement, where anything was possible.

Question and Answer

Q: What was the most popular toy of the 80s?

A: It's hard to pick just one, but some of the most popular toys of the 80s included Cabbage Patch Kids, Transformers, and the Nintendo Entertainment System.

Q: Did toy stores in the 80s have Black Friday sales?

A: While Black Friday as we know it today didn't exist in the 80s, many toy stores did have special sales and promotions around the holidays.

Q: What was the most expensive toy of the 80s?

A: One of the most expensive toys of the 80s was the Optimus Prime Transformer, which retailed for around $25. This may not sound like much today, but adjusted for inflation, it would be around $70.

Q: Are any of the toy stores from the 80s still around today?

A: While many of the big chains like Toys R Us and Kay-Bee Toys have closed their doors, there are still plenty of local toy stores around that capture the spirit of the 80s.
