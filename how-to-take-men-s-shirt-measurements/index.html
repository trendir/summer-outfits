How To Take Men s Shirt Measurements A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of buying shirts that never fit properly? Do you find yourself constantly returning shirts because they're too tight or too loose? Taking accurate measurements is the key to finding the perfect fit. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the process of how to take men's shirt measurements.

It can be frustrating when you can't find a shirt that fits comfortably, especially when shopping online. Taking men's shirt measurements is essential when shopping for shirts online or getting a custom-made shirt. It can be daunting to measure yourself, but it's worth the effort to have a well-fitting shirt.

Before we dive into the steps, make sure you have a measuring tape, pen, and paper to record your measurements.

To take your men's shirt measurements, follow these steps:

Step 1: Neck Measurement

Start by measuring your neck. Place the measuring tape around the base of your neck, where the collar would normally sit. Make sure the tape is snug but not too tight. Record the measurement in inches.

Step 2: Shoulder Measurement

Next, measure your shoulder width. Place the measuring tape at the base of your neck and measure across to the edge of your shoulder. Record the measurement in inches.

Step 3: Chest Measurement

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, making sure the tape is level and snug but not too tight. Record the measurement in inches.

Step 4: Waist Measurement

Measure around your natural waistline, which is about an inch above your belly button. Make sure the tape is level and snug but not too tight. Record the measurement in inches.

Step 5: Sleeve Measurement

Measure from the base of your neck, across your shoulder, and down to your wrist. Make sure your arm is relaxed at your side when measuring. Record the measurement in inches.

Step 6: Shirt Length Measurement

Measure from the base of your neck down to the length you prefer your shirt to be. Record the measurement in inches.

Once you have all of your measurements, you can use them to find the perfect fitting shirt or to get a custom-made shirt.

Personal Experience

As someone who has struggled to find shirts that fit comfortably, taking accurate measurements has been a game-changer. I used to settle for shirts that were either too tight or too loose, but now I can confidently shop for shirts online or get them custom-made. It's a simple process that anyone can do, and it makes all the difference in finding the perfect fit.

Tips for Taking Accurate Measurements

Here are some tips to keep in mind when taking men's shirt measurements:

  • Wear form-fitting clothing when measuring.
  • Make sure the measuring tape is snug but not too tight.
  • Measure yourself in front of a mirror to ensure the tape is straight.
  • Ask someone to help you take your measurements for more accuracy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are some common mistakes to avoid when taking men's shirt measurements:

  • Measuring over bulky clothing.
  • Not holding the measuring tape straight.
  • Recording measurements in the wrong units (inches vs. centimeters).
  • Not measuring yourself in the correct posture.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I take my measurements?

A: It's a good idea to take your measurements every six months to ensure accuracy, especially if you have experienced weight fluctuations.

Q: How do I measure sleeve length if I have longer arms?

A: If you have longer arms, you can add an inch or two to the sleeve measurement to ensure a proper fit.

Q: Should I measure myself or ask someone to help me?

A: It's best to have someone help you take your measurements for more accuracy.

Q: How do I know if I'm measuring myself correctly?

A: You can check your measurements by measuring yourself a second time or asking someone to take your measurements for comparison.

Conclusion of How to Take Men's Shirt Measurements

Taking accurate men's shirt measurements is the key to finding the perfect fit. With these simple steps, you can confidently shop for shirts online or get them custom-made. Remember to wear form-fitting clothing, keep the measuring tape snug but not too tight, and ask someone for help if needed. Happy shopping!
