What Is A Slack Shared Channel

Written by Bardi Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to communicate with multiple teams or organizations in one place, a Slack Shared Channel might be the answer for you. This tool allows you to share conversations, files, and other information with members of other Slack workspaces, making it easier to collaborate with people outside of your immediate team.

Do you find it difficult to keep track of conversations spread across multiple channels and workspaces? Are you tired of constantly switching between different apps to communicate with different teams? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with the same issues, which is why Slack Shared Channels are becoming increasingly popular.

A Slack Shared Channel is a channel that allows members of different Slack workspaces to communicate with each other in one place. It's like having a virtual conference room where everyone can gather to discuss ideas, share files, and collaborate on projects.

My Experience with Slack Shared Channels

As a freelance writer, I often work with clients who use different project management tools and communication apps. Before I started using Slack Shared Channels, I found it difficult to keep track of conversations and files across multiple platforms. But now, I can easily communicate with all of my clients in one place, which saves me time and helps me stay organized.

Slack Shared Channels have also been helpful when collaborating with other writers and editors. Instead of sending emails back and forth or using different messaging apps, we can all communicate in one place and stay on the same page.

How to Set Up a Slack Shared Channel

To set up a Slack Shared Channel, you'll need to be an admin or owner of a workspace. Here are the steps:

  1. Click on the workspace name in the top left corner of the screen.
  2. Select "Add Channel" from the drop-down menu.
  3. Choose "Shared Channel" from the options.
  4. Select the workspace you want to share the channel with.
  5. Choose a name for the channel and click "Create Channel."
  6. Invite members from the other workspace to join the channel.

Benefits of Using Slack Shared Channels

One of the biggest benefits of using Slack Shared Channels is that it eliminates the need for multiple apps and channels. Instead of switching between different platforms to communicate with different teams, you can do it all in one place. This saves time and makes it easier to collaborate with people outside of your immediate team.

Another benefit is that it allows for more seamless collaboration between teams. Instead of having to constantly update different teams on project progress, you can communicate with everyone in one place. This helps ensure that everyone is on the same page and reduces the risk of miscommunication.

Tips for Using Slack Shared Channels

Here are a few tips for using Slack Shared Channels:

  1. Set clear guidelines for communication and file sharing.
  2. Use appropriate tags and emojis to help organize conversations.
  3. Regularly check the channel for updates and respond in a timely manner.
  4. Be respectful of other teams' time and resources.
