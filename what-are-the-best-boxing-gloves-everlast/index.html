Choosing the Right Size

When selecting a pair of Everlast boxing gloves, it is important to choose the right size. Gloves that are too small can be uncomfortable and may not provide enough protection for the hands. Gloves that are too large can be difficult to control and may cause unnecessary fatigue.

To determine the right size, measure the circumference of your dominant hand around the knuckles. Use this measurement to select the appropriate size from the Everlast sizing chart. Keep in mind that different styles of gloves may fit differently, so be sure to try on multiple pairs to find the best fit.

Material and Quality

The material and quality of the gloves are also important factors to consider. Everlast offers gloves made from a variety of materials, including synthetic leather, genuine leather, and vinyl. Genuine leather gloves are generally considered to be the highest quality, but they can also be quite expensive. Synthetic leather gloves are a good alternative and are often more affordable.

It is also important to consider the quality of the gloves. Look for gloves that are well-constructed with reinforced stitching and high-quality padding. This will ensure that the gloves last through frequent use and provide adequate protection for the hands.

Types of Everlast Gloves

Everlast offers a variety of gloves for different types of training and fighting. Some of the most popular types of Everlast gloves include:

  • Training gloves
  • Sparring gloves
  • Bag gloves
  • Competition gloves

Taking Care of Your Gloves

Once you have selected the best pair of Everlast boxing gloves for your needs, it is important to take care of them properly. This will help to extend the life of the gloves and ensure they continue to provide adequate protection for the hands.

After each use, wipe down the gloves with a clean cloth to remove any sweat or dirt. Avoid leaving the gloves in direct sunlight or extreme temperatures, as this can damage the material. Additionally, be sure to store the gloves in a dry, ventilated area to prevent mold and bacteria growth.

Question and Answer

Q. How do I know if my gloves fit properly?

A. Your gloves should fit snugly without feeling too tight. Your fingers should be able to move freely inside the gloves, and there should be no gaps between your fingers and the gloves. Additionally, the gloves should not slip around on your hands during use.

Q. How often should I replace my boxing gloves?

A. The frequency with which you should replace your boxing gloves depends on how often you use them. Generally, gloves that are used frequently will need to be replaced every 6-12 months. However, if you notice any signs of wear or tear, such as holes or frayed stitching, it may be time to replace your gloves sooner.

Q. Can I use the same gloves for sparring and bag work?

A. While it is possible to use the same gloves for both sparring and bag work, it is generally not recommended. Sparring gloves are designed to provide more protection for both you and your sparring partner, while bag gloves are designed to be more durable for frequent use on a heavy bag. Using the wrong type of gloves for a particular activity can increase the risk of injury.
