Why Did The Chinese Wear Pigtails

Have you ever wondered why men in traditional Chinese attire wear a long braided hairstyle known as a pigtail? While this hairstyle is no longer widely worn in China today, it was once a common sight during the Qing dynasty. In this article, we will explore the history and significance of this hairstyle.

During the Qing dynasty, men in China were required by law to wear a queue, also known as a pigtail. The queue was a long braid of hair that was worn down the back of the head. This law was enforced by the Manchu rulers of the Qing dynasty, who wanted to distinguish themselves from the Han Chinese. The queue became a symbol of loyalty to the Qing dynasty and was seen as a way to show that a man was a law-abiding citizen.

The Qing dynasty ruled China from 1644 to 1912, and during this time, the queue became an important part of Chinese culture. It was worn by men of all ages and social classes and was seen as a symbol of identity and loyalty. However, not all Chinese men were happy with the requirement to wear the queue, and there were many who resented the Manchu rulers for imposing this law on them.

In summary, the pigtail or queue was a mandatory hairstyle worn by men in China during the Qing dynasty. It was a symbol of loyalty and identity, but it was also a source of resentment for many Chinese men who saw it as a symbol of oppression.

The Significance of the Pigtail

The pigtail was a symbol of loyalty to the Qing dynasty and was seen as a way to distinguish the ruling Manchu class from the Han Chinese. However, it was also a way for the Qing dynasty to exert control over the Han Chinese, who made up the majority of the population. By requiring men to wear the pigtail, the Qing dynasty was able to demonstrate its power and authority.

The End of the Pigtail

The pigtail was abolished in China in 1911 at the end of the Qing dynasty. The new government that came to power after the fall of the Qing dynasty saw the pigtail as a symbol of oppression and wanted to modernize China by getting rid of old traditions. Today, the pigtail is no longer worn in China, but it remains a significant symbol of China's past.
