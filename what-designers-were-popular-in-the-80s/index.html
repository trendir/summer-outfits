Exploring The Popular Designers Of The 80S

Written by Eveline Aug 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you remember the vibrant and bold fashion trends of the 80s? From neon colors to oversized clothing, the 80s was a decade of statement-making looks. But what about the designers behind these iconic styles? Let's take a closer look at the popular designers of the 80s and how they influenced the fashion industry.

The 80s was a time of change and experimentation in the fashion industry. Designers were pushing the boundaries with their designs and creating new trends that were both bold and innovative. However, with so many designers emerging during this time, it can be overwhelming to know who to look out for.

Some of the most popular designers of the 80s include Giorgio Armani, Calvin Klein, and Ralph Lauren. These designers were known for their clean lines, minimalistic designs, and high-quality fabrics. They were the epitome of the "power dressing" trend that dominated the 80s, where women wanted to dress in a way that exuded authority and confidence.

In addition to these designers, there were also a number of influential designers who made their mark on the fashion industry during the 80s. Designers like Vivienne Westwood, Jean Paul Gaultier, and Thierry Mugler were known for their avant-garde designs and pushing the boundaries of fashion. Their designs were edgy, rebellious, and often featured unconventional materials.

My Personal Experience with 80s Designers

As someone who grew up in the 80s, I vividly remember the impact that these designers had on the fashion industry. I remember seeing the iconic Calvin Klein ads featuring Brooke Shields and feeling inspired by the simplicity and elegance of his designs. I also remember being intrigued by the bold and daring designs of Vivienne Westwood, who was known for her punk-inspired aesthetic.

Overall, the designers of the 80s were incredibly influential and left a lasting impression on the fashion industry. Their bold and innovative designs continue to inspire designers to this day.

The Legacy of 80s Designers

While the 80s may be known for its bold and daring fashion trends, the designers of that era were also responsible for ushering in a new era of fashion. They brought minimalism and simplicity to the forefront of fashion, which has continued to influence designers to this day. Additionally, their use of unconventional materials and willingness to push the boundaries of fashion has paved the way for future designers to experiment and innovate.

The Impact of Giorgio Armani

One designer who had a significant impact on the fashion industry during the 80s was Giorgio Armani. His designs were known for their clean lines, neutral colors, and high-quality fabrics. Armani was responsible for popularizing the "power dressing" trend that was popular during the 80s, which involved women dressing in a way that exuded authority and confidence.

How to Incorporate 80s Design Trends Today

While the 80s may be known for its bold and daring fashion trends, there are ways to incorporate these trends into your wardrobe today. For example, you can try pairing oversized clothing with more streamlined pieces to create a modern take on the trend. Additionally, you can experiment with neon colors and bold prints to add a pop of color to your outfit.

The Enduring Influence of 80s Designers

The designers of the 80s continue to influence the fashion industry to this day. Their willingness to experiment and take risks has inspired future generations of designers to push the boundaries of fashion. From the clean lines of Giorgio Armani to the rebellious designs of Vivienne Westwood, the legacy of 80s designers will continue to live on.

Conclusion of what designers were popular in the 80s

The designers of the 80s were some of the most influential and innovative designers in the fashion industry. From Giorgio Armani to Vivienne Westwood, these designers left a lasting impact on the fashion industry and continue to inspire designers to this day. By incorporating some of the trends and styles of the 80s into your wardrobe, you can pay homage to these iconic designers and their enduring legacy.
