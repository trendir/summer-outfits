Why Do Indian Brides Wear Red

Written by Daniel Oct 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors, intricate rituals, and lavish celebrations. One of the most striking aspects of an Indian wedding is the bride's attire, which is usually a red wedding dress known as a "lehenga" or "saree." The color red is considered auspicious in Indian culture and has been a traditional choice for brides for centuries. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons behind why Indian brides wear red on their wedding day.

The tradition of wearing red on the wedding day is deeply rooted in Indian culture and holds significant meaning. For many Indians, marriage is not just a union between two individuals, but also a union of two families. The wedding day is seen as a momentous occasion, with the bride and groom being the center of attention. With so much importance placed on the wedding day, it is no wonder that brides want to look their best and dress in a way that reflects the significance of the occasion.

There are several reasons why Indian brides wear red on their wedding day. One of the most common beliefs is that red is a symbol of fertility, love, and purity. In Hinduism, the color red is associated with the goddess of strength and power, and is believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits. The red color of the wedding dress is also said to represent the rising sun, which symbolizes a new beginning and a bright future for the newlyweds.

In addition to its symbolic significance, the color red is also considered to be a flattering color for Indian skin tones. The bold and vibrant hue of the red wedding dress complements the golden undertones of Indian skin, making the bride look radiant and beautiful on her special day.

Personal Experience

As an Indian woman, I have always been fascinated by the tradition of wearing red on the wedding day. When I got married, I knew that I wanted to wear a red lehenga, not just because it was tradition, but also because I felt it was the most beautiful and flattering color for me. Seeing myself in the mirror on my wedding day, wearing a red lehenga and adorned with gold jewelry, made me feel like a true Indian bride.

The Significance of Red in Indian Culture

Red is a color that holds great significance in Indian culture. It is believed to represent passion, love, and prosperity. In addition to its use in weddings, the color red is also seen in other aspects of Indian culture, such as festivals, religious ceremonies, and even everyday clothing. Women often wear red sarees or kurtas on special occasions, and the color is also used in traditional Indian art, such as henna designs and rangolis.

The Evolution of Indian Bridal Fashion

While the tradition of wearing red on the wedding day has remained constant over the years, the style and design of the wedding dress have evolved with time. Today, Indian brides have a wide variety of options when it comes to choosing their wedding attire. The traditional red lehenga is still a popular choice, but brides can also opt for other colors such as pink, green, or blue, depending on their personal preference. In addition, modern brides often incorporate western elements into their wedding attire, such as wearing a gown for the reception or accessorizing with diamond jewelry.

Tips for Choosing a Red Wedding Dress

If you are an Indian bride-to-be and are considering wearing a red wedding dress, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a shade of red that complements your skin tone
  • Consider the style and design of the dress, and make sure it is comfortable to wear
  • Accessorize with gold jewelry, which complements the red color
  • Keep the makeup simple and elegant, to avoid overpowering the dress

Personal Reflections

As an Indian woman, I feel a deep sense of pride in the tradition of wearing red on the wedding day. The color red holds so much significance in Indian culture, and I believe that wearing a red wedding dress is a way for brides to honor their heritage and embrace their identity. Whether it is a traditional red lehenga or a modern fusion gown, the red wedding dress will always hold a special place in the hearts of Indian brides.

Question and Answer

Q: Is red the only color worn by Indian brides?

A: No, Indian brides have a wide variety of options when it comes to choosing their wedding attire. While red is the most traditional color, brides can also opt for other colors such as pink, green, or blue.

Q: Do Indian brides wear a particular type of jewelry with their red wedding dress?

A: Yes, Indian brides often wear gold jewelry with their red wedding dress. This includes a necklace, earrings, bangles, and a maang tikka (a type of headpiece).

Q: What is the significance of mehndi (henna) in Indian weddings?

A: Mehndi is a traditional form of body art that is often applied to the hands and feet of the bride before the wedding day. It is believed to bring good luck and is a way for the bride to express her creativity and individuality.

Q: What are some other traditions associated with Indian weddings?

A: Indian weddings are full of rich traditions and rituals, such as the exchange of garlands between the bride and groom, the tying of the mangalsutra (a sacred necklace), and the seven vows taken by the couple during the wedding ceremony.

Conclusion of Why Do Indian Brides Wear Red

The tradition of wearing red on the wedding day is an important aspect of Indian culture and holds deep symbolic meaning. Whether it is a traditional red lehenga or a modern fusion gown, the color red will always remain a popular choice for Indian brides. By embracing this tradition, brides are able to honor their heritage and celebrate their identity on one of the most important days of their lives.
