How To Do Fashion Designing Courses After 12Th A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you fascinated by the world of fashion and aspire to become a fashion designer? If you have just completed your 12th grade and are wondering how to pursue a career in fashion designing, this guide is for you. In this blog post, we will take you through the steps you need to take to get started with fashion designing courses after 12th.

As a student, you may face a lot of confusion and uncertainty about your career. One of the biggest challenges is to choose the right course that aligns with your interests and future goals. When it comes to fashion designing, there are various options available to students after 12th. However, choosing the right course can be a daunting task.

Step 1: Research Your Options

The first step towards pursuing a career in fashion designing after 12th is to research your options thoroughly. There are various undergraduate and diploma courses available in fashion designing that you can choose from. Some of the popular ones include Bachelor of Design in Fashion Design, Bachelor of Fashion Technology, Diploma in Fashion Designing, and Certificate Courses in Fashion Designing.

You can check the eligibility criteria, course duration, fees, and admission process of different fashion designing courses and shortlist the ones that suit your requirements. You can also read reviews of the courses and colleges online.

Step 2: Choose the Right College

Choosing the right college is as important as choosing the right course. You need to consider various factors such as the college's reputation, faculty, infrastructure, placements, and location before finalizing a college. You can visit the college's website, attend college fairs, and talk to alumni and current students to get an idea of the college's culture and environment.

It is also important to note that some colleges may have specific entrance exams or portfolio requirements for admission. Make sure you check the admission criteria of the colleges you are interested in before applying.

Step 3: Prepare for Entrance Exams and Interviews

Once you have shortlisted the colleges and courses you are interested in, you need to start preparing for entrance exams and interviews. Most fashion designing colleges conduct their own entrance exams, and a few also accept national-level entrance exams such as NIFT, NID, and UCEED.

Entrance exams usually test your creativity, design skills, and general knowledge. You can prepare for them by practicing sketching, reading fashion magazines, and staying updated with the latest fashion trends. You may also have to appear for a personal interview or portfolio review as part of the admission process.

Step 4: Build Your Portfolio

A portfolio is a collection of your best design work and projects that showcase your creativity and skills. It is an important component of your application process and can make or break your chances of getting admitted to a good college. You can start building your portfolio by taking up design projects, internships, and participating in fashion shows and competitions.

Make sure your portfolio reflects your personal style, skills, and creativity. You can also seek feedback from your teachers, peers, and industry professionals to improve your portfolio.

Question and Answer Section

Q. What are the career options after completing a fashion designing course?

A. After completing a fashion designing course, you can work as a fashion designer, fashion stylist, fashion merchandiser, textile designer, fashion journalist, or fashion buyer. You can also start your own fashion label or work as a freelancer.

Q. Is it necessary to have a background in art or design to pursue a career in fashion designing?

A. While having a background in art or design can be an advantage, it is not a mandatory requirement to pursue a career in fashion designing. You can learn the necessary skills and techniques through a fashion designing course and develop your creativity through practice and hard work.

Q. What are some of the skills required to become a successful fashion designer?

A. Some of the essential skills required to become a successful fashion designer include creativity, design skills, knowledge of fabrics and textiles, attention to detail, communication skills, and the ability to work under pressure and deadlines.

Q. Can I pursue a fashion designing course through distance learning?

A. Yes, there are some colleges and universities that offer fashion designing courses through distance learning. However, it is important to note that distance learning may not provide the same level of practical training and exposure as a regular course.

Conclusion

Pursuing a career in fashion designing after 12th can be an exciting and rewarding experience. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can make an informed decision about the course and college that suits your interests and career goals. Remember to stay updated with the latest fashion trends, work hard, and stay true to your creative vision to succeed in this competitive industry.
