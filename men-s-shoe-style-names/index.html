Men s Shoe Style Names A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Shoes are more than just something that covers your feet. They can make or break an outfit, and knowing the different styles of men's shoes can be overwhelming. With so many names and terms to remember, it's easy to get confused. In this guide, we'll break down some of the most common men's shoe style names and make it easier for you to navigate the world of footwear.

Have you ever found yourself in a shoe store and not known what to ask for? Or have you been browsing online and come across a shoe that you like but don't know what it's called? The world of men's shoe style names can be confusing, but it doesn't have to be. Knowing the basics can help you make informed decisions when shopping for shoes, and can make it easier to express what you're looking for to a salesperson or tailor.

Dress Shoes

Dress shoes are the most formal type of men's shoe. They're typically made of leather and are designed to be worn with suits or other formal attire. The most common types of dress shoes include:

Oxford shoes: These are the quintessential dress shoe. They have a closed lacing system, meaning that the eyelets are stitched under the vamp (the front part of the shoe). This gives them a sleek, streamlined look that's perfect for formal occasions.

Derby shoes: Derby shoes are similar to Oxfords, but they have an open lacing system. This means that the eyelets are stitched on top of the vamp, which gives them a slightly more casual look. They're still formal enough to wear with a suit, but can also be dressed down with chinos or jeans.

Monk strap shoes: These shoes have a buckle instead of laces, which gives them a unique look. They come in single, double, and even triple monk strap styles, and can be worn with both formal and casual outfits.

Casual Shoes

Casual shoes are the most versatile type of men's shoe. They can be worn with everything from shorts to suits, and come in a wide range of styles. Some of the most popular casual shoe styles include:

Sneakers: Sneakers are the ultimate casual shoe. They're comfortable, practical, and come in a wide range of colors and styles. They can be worn with everything from jeans to suits, and are a great way to add a pop of color to an outfit.

Loafers: Loafers are slip-on shoes that are perfect for casual wear. They come in a variety of styles, including penny loafers and tassel loafers, and can be worn with everything from shorts to chinos.

Boat shoes: Boat shoes are a classic summer shoe. They're typically made of leather or canvas and have a rubber sole that's designed to provide traction on wet surfaces. They're perfect for wearing with shorts or rolled-up chinos.

Boots

Boots are a great way to add some variety to your footwear collection. They come in a range of styles, from rugged work boots to sleek Chelsea boots. Some popular men's boot styles include:

Chukka boots: Chukka boots are ankle-length boots that are typically made of suede or leather. They have a simple, understated design that makes them a great choice for casual wear.

Chelsea boots: Chelsea boots are another type of ankle-length boot. They have an elastic panel on the side that makes them easy to slip on and off, and are often worn with slim-fitting pants or jeans.

Work boots: Work boots are designed to be sturdy and durable. They're often made of leather and have a thick sole that provides traction and support. They're a great choice for outdoor activities or for jobs that require a lot of standing or walking.

Conclusion

Knowing the different types of men's shoe style names can be overwhelming at first, but it's worth taking the time to learn. By understanding what different styles are called, you can make more informed decisions when shopping for shoes and can better express what you're looking for to a salesperson or tailor. Whether you're in the market for dress shoes, casual shoes, or boots, there's a style out there that's perfect for you.

Q&A:

Q: What's the difference between Oxfords and Derbys?

A: The main difference is in the lacing system. Oxfords have a closed lacing system, while Derbys have an open lacing system. This gives Oxfords a sleeker, more formal look, while Derbys have a slightly more casual feel.

Q: Can you wear sneakers with a suit?

A: It depends on the suit and the sneakers. If you're wearing a formal suit, it's probably best to stick with dress shoes. However, if you're wearing a more casual suit (like a linen suit), you could get away with wearing sneakers. Just make sure they're clean and in good condition.

Q: What's the difference between loafers and moccasins?

A: Loafers and moccasins are similar in that they're both slip-on shoes. However, moccasins are typically made of softer, more flexible leather and have a distinctive stitched toe. Loafers, on the other hand, are often made of stiffer leather and have a more structured design.

Q: Are work boots only for work?

A: No, work boots can be worn for a variety of activities. They're great for hiking, camping, or any other outdoor activity where you need a sturdy, supportive shoe. They can also be worn for casual occasions, especially if you're going for a rugged, outdoorsy look.

Conclusion of Men's Shoe Style Names

Understanding men's shoe style names can be a daunting task, but it's essential if you want to make informed decisions when it comes to footwear. By familiarizing yourself with the different types of dress shoes, casual shoes, and boots, you can make sure you're always putting your best foot forward (pun intended). So next time you're in the market for a new pair of shoes, remember to keep these style names in mind!
