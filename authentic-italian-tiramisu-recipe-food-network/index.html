The Recipe Instructions

Ingredients:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese
  • 1/4 cup strong brewed coffee or espresso
  • 2 tablespoons rum
  • 24 ladyfingers
  • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until well blended. Whisk in milk and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  2. Transfer mixture to a large bowl and let cool to room temperature. Add mascarpone cheese and whisk until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream and vanilla until soft peaks form. Fold whipped cream into mascarpone mixture.
  4. In a small bowl, combine coffee and rum.
  5. Dip ladyfingers in coffee mixture and arrange in a single layer in a 9x13 inch dish.
  6. Spoon half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture.
  7. Sift cocoa powder over the top and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to ensure your tiramisu turns out perfectly:

  • Use high-quality ingredients, especially the mascarpone cheese.
  • Do not over-soak the ladyfingers in the coffee mixture, as they will become too soggy and fall apart.
  • Refrigerate the tiramisu for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.
  • Sift cocoa powder over the top before serving for a decorative touch.

Authentic Italian Tiramisu Recipe Food Network in Italy

In Italy, tiramisu is a beloved dessert that is often served at special occasions and family gatherings. The recipe varies slightly by region, but the basic ingredients remain the same.

The key to making an authentic Italian tiramisu is to use high-quality ingredients, especially the mascarpone cheese. Additionally, the ladyfingers should be dipped in strong coffee or espresso, and a touch of rum can be added for extra flavor.

How to Store Authentic Italian Tiramisu Recipe Food Network

If you have any leftovers of your tiramisu, you can store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Cover the dish with plastic wrap or aluminum foil to prevent it from drying out.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I substitute another type of cheese for mascarpone in the recipe?

A: While it is possible to substitute cream cheese or ricotta cheese for mascarpone, the flavor and texture of the tiramisu may be affected.


Q: Can I use decaf coffee in the recipe?

A: Yes, you can use decaf coffee or even hot chocolate in place of the coffee and rum mixture.


Q: How many servings does this recipe make?

A: This recipe makes 8-10 servings, depending on the size of the portion.


Q: Can I freeze tiramisu?

A: It is not recommended to freeze tiramisu, as the texture and flavor may be affected.

By using high-quality ingredients, dipping the ladyfingers in a coffee and rum mixture, and refrigerating the dessert before serving, you can make a delicious and authentic tiramisu that will impress your guests.
