How To Wear Ankle Boots With Dresses A Relaxed Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old boring outfit choices? Do you want to spice up your wardrobe with something new? Look no further than ankle boots and dresses! But we know it can be a bit daunting to figure out how to wear ankle boots with dresses, especially if you've never tried the trend before. Don't worry, we've got you covered with this relaxed guide.

One of the main concerns when it comes to wearing ankle boots with dresses is how to balance the proportions of the outfit. You don't want to look too top-heavy or bottom-heavy, so it's important to find the right balance. Another concern is how to choose the right type of ankle boot for your dress. But fear not, we'll address all of these pain points in the following paragraphs.

First things first, let's talk about the basics of how to wear ankle boots with dresses. The key is to make sure the dress and ankle boots complement each other. If you have a flowy or loose-fitting dress, pair it with a sleek and fitted ankle boot. If you have a more form-fitting dress, opt for a looser, more relaxed ankle boot. The idea is to balance out the proportions of the outfit.

Now that we've covered the basics, let's dive deeper into the specifics of how to wear ankle boots with dresses.

Pairing Ankle Boots with a Short Dress

If you're wearing a short dress, ankle boots are the perfect choice to add some edge to your outfit. Choose a pair of ankle boots with a chunky heel or platform to elongate your legs and balance out the shorter hemline of the dress. For a more casual look, pair your ankle boots with a denim or leather jacket.

Pairing Ankle Boots with a Midi Dress

If you're wearing a midi dress, ankle boots can help you transition your outfit from summer to fall. Choose a pair of ankle boots in a neutral color, like black or brown, and pair them with a midi dress in a bright or bold color. This will create a striking contrast and make your outfit pop. For an even more stylish look, add a belt around your waist to define your silhouette.

Choosing the Right Type of Ankle Boot

When it comes to choosing the right type of ankle boot for your dress, it's important to consider the style of the dress. If you're wearing a dress with a bohemian or vintage feel, opt for ankle boots with fringe or embroidery. If you're wearing a more modern or sleek dress, choose ankle boots with a clean, minimal design.

Accessorizing Your Ankle Boot and Dress Outfit

Accessorizing your ankle boot and dress outfit is key to making it look complete. For a more formal look, pair your ankle boots with a statement necklace or earrings. For a more casual look, add a scarf or hat. And don't forget to add some texture to your outfit with a belt or patterned tights.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear ankle boots with a maxi dress?

A: Yes, you can definitely wear ankle boots with a maxi dress! Just make sure the ankle boots are visible enough, because the point is to show off the boots.

Q: Can I wear ankle boots with a printed dress?

A: Absolutely! Just make sure the ankle boots complement the print of the dress. For example, if you're wearing a floral dress, pair it with ankle boots in a neutral color.

Q: Can I wear ankle boots with a formal dress?

A: Yes, you can wear ankle boots with a formal dress, but it's important to choose the right type of ankle boot. Opt for a sleek, minimal design in a neutral color like black or nude.

Q: Can I wear ankle boots with a long-sleeved dress?

A: Absolutely! In fact, ankle boots can help balance out the proportions of a long-sleeved dress. Just make sure the ankle boots don't cut off at an awkward point on your leg.

Conclusion of how to wear ankle boots with dresses

By following these simple tips, you'll be a pro at how to wear ankle boots with dresses in no time. Remember to balance out the proportions of your outfit, choose the right type of ankle boot for your dress, and accessorize to complete the look. There's no need to be intimidated by this trend, so go ahead and give it a try!
