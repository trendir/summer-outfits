Indulge In Guilt Free Chocolate With These 10 Best Ethical Chocolate Brands

Written by Eveline Sep 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a chocolate lover who wants to enjoy this sweet treat without harming the environment or exploiting workers? If so, you're in luck because there are many ethical chocolate brands that offer delicious and sustainable options. In this article, we'll explore the 10 best ethical chocolate brands that you can indulge in without any guilt.

Our target is to provide information about the 10 best ethical chocolate brands available in the market. We want to help readers make an informed decision when it comes to choosing chocolate that is both tasty and ethical.

To summarize, we'll be discussing the 10 best ethical chocolate brands and their sustainable practices. We'll also touch on the fair trade and organic certifications that these brands have received. By the end of this article, you'll have a better understanding of the ethical chocolate industry and will be able to make a more informed decision when it comes to buying chocolate.

1. Alter Eco Chocolate

My personal experience with Alter Eco Chocolate has been nothing short of amazing. Not only do they offer delicious flavors like dark chocolate with quinoa, but they also prioritize sustainability and fair trade practices. Alter Eco Chocolate is USDA organic certified and is committed to using only the highest quality ingredients. They also use eco-friendly packaging and are a certified B-corporation.

2. Divine Chocolate

Divine Chocolate is a company that is 44% owned by cocoa farmers, which means they have a direct say in how the company is run. This Fairtrade certified company offers a range of delicious chocolate flavors and is committed to empowering cocoa farmers by paying them fairly for their work. They also use sustainable packaging and are a certified B-corporation.

3. Endangered Species Chocolate

Endangered Species Chocolate is committed to using ethically sourced cocoa and donating 10% of their net profits to organizations that help protect endangered species. They are also Rainforest Alliance certified and offer a range of delicious flavors, including dark chocolate with sea salt and almond.

4. Equal Exchange Chocolate

Equal Exchange Chocolate is a worker-owned cooperative that is committed to fair trade practices and using organic ingredients. They offer a range of delicious chocolate flavors and are committed to supporting small farmers and co-ops around the world.

5. Green & Black's Chocolate

Green & Black's Chocolate is committed to using ethically sourced cocoa and is certified organic by the USDA. They offer a range of delicious flavors, including dark chocolate with sea salt and milk chocolate with toffee. They also use sustainable packaging and are a certified B-corporation.

6. Lake Champlain Chocolates

Lake Champlain Chocolates is committed to using fair trade cocoa and organic ingredients. They offer a range of delicious flavors and are committed to using sustainable packaging. They are also a certified B-corporation.

7. Taza Chocolate

Taza Chocolate is committed to using organic, direct trade cocoa and stone-grinding it for a unique, bold flavor. They offer a range of delicious flavors, including chocolate-covered almonds and coconut. They are also a certified B-corporation.

8. Theo Chocolate

Theo Chocolate is committed to using organic, fair trade cocoa and supporting small farmers around the world. They offer a range of delicious flavors, including dark chocolate with cherries and almond butter cups. They are also a certified B-corporation.

9. Tony's Chocolonely

Tony's Chocolonely is committed to ending slavery in the cocoa industry and uses only ethically sourced cocoa. They offer a range of delicious flavors, including milk chocolate with hazelnuts and dark chocolate with pretzel and toffee. They are also a certified B-corporation.

10. Videri Chocolate

Videri Chocolate is committed to using organic and direct trade cocoa and has a bean-to-bar process that ensures the highest quality chocolate. They offer a range of delicious flavors, including dark chocolate with sea salt and chocolate-covered espresso beans.

Why should you choose ethical chocolate brands?

By choosing ethical chocolate brands, you are supporting sustainable and fair trade practices in the cocoa industry. This means that cocoa farmers are paid fairly for their work and are not exploited. Ethical chocolate brands also prioritize sustainability by using eco-friendly packaging and reducing their carbon footprint.

How can you find ethical chocolate brands?

You can find ethical chocolate brands by looking for fair trade and organic certifications on the packaging. You can also do your own research online and look for brands that prioritize sustainability and fair trade practices.

Conclusion of 10 best ethical chocolate brands

There are many ethical chocolate brands available in the market that offer delicious and sustainable options. By choosing to support these brands, you are making a positive impact on the environment and supporting fair trade practices in the cocoa industry. So go ahead and indulge in guilt-free chocolate from these 10 best ethical chocolate brands!

Question and Answer

Q: What does it mean when a chocolate brand is certified organic?

A: When a chocolate brand is certified organic, it means that the ingredients used to make the chocolate are grown without the use of synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms (GMOs).

Q: What does it mean when a chocolate brand is certified fair trade?

A: When a chocolate brand is certified fair trade, it means that the cocoa farmers who grow the cocoa beans used in the chocolate are paid fairly for their work and are not exploited.

Q: What does it mean when a chocolate brand is certified Rainforest Alliance?

A: When a chocolate brand is certified Rainforest Alliance, it means that the cocoa used in the chocolate is grown in a way that protects the environment and supports the well-being of workers and their communities.

Q: What is a B-corporation?

A: A B-corporation is a type of company that is committed to social and environmental responsibility. B-corporations are required to meet certain standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency.
