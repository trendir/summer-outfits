Why Do Indians Wear Red Bracelets

Written by Daniel Jul 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered why Indians wear red bracelets? You may have seen this tradition in many Indian movies or in real life, and you may have wondered what it represents. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons why Indians wear red bracelets and its significance in their culture.

The tradition of wearing red bracelets is deeply rooted in Indian culture and is considered to be auspicious. It is believed that these bracelets protect the wearer from negative energies and evil spirits. Indians believe that wearing a red bracelet brings good fortune, prosperity, and success in life.

The red bracelet is called a 'Mauli' or a 'Kalava' in Hindi, and it is made of red thread. The thread is usually tied around the wrist of the person by a priest during a religious ceremony or a ritual. The thread is tied after it has been blessed by a priest, and it is believed that the blessings of the priest are transferred to the person wearing the bracelet.

The Significance of Wearing a Red Bracelet

When I was a child, my grandmother used to tie a red bracelet around my wrist every year during a religious ceremony. She usually tied it while chanting some mantras and pouring holy water on my head. I used to love wearing the bracelet as it made me feel special and protected.

The significance of wearing a red bracelet is not just limited to protection and good fortune; it also represents a person's connection with their culture and traditions. It is a way of showing respect for one's cultural heritage and beliefs.

The Role of Religion in Wearing a Red Bracelet

Religion plays a significant role in the tradition of wearing a red bracelet. In Hinduism, the bracelet is tied during religious ceremonies and is believed to protect the person from negative energies and evil spirits. In Buddhism, the bracelet is worn as a reminder to practice mindfulness and stay connected to the present moment.

The Science Behind Wearing a Red Bracelet

There is no scientific evidence to support the belief that wearing a red bracelet brings good fortune or protects a person from negative energies. However, the placebo effect of wearing the bracelet can be significant. If a person believes that wearing a red bracelet will bring them good fortune, they may feel more confident and positive, which could lead to success and prosperity.

How to Make a Red Bracelet

Making a red bracelet is easy and can be done at home. All you need is a red thread, scissors, and some patience. Here's how you can make a red bracelet:

  1. Cut a piece of red thread that is long enough to go around your wrist.
  2. Tie a knot at one end of the thread.
  3. Start braiding the thread, making sure to keep it tight.
  4. Once you reach the end of the thread, tie a knot to secure it.
  5. Your red bracelet is ready to wear!
