1. Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier - "The Thrilla in Manila"

I remember watching this fight with my grandfather, who was a big boxing fan. It was the third and final fight between two of the greatest heavyweight boxers of all time, Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight took place in 1975, in Manila, Philippines, and it was one of the most brutal and intense fights in boxing history.

Ali and Frazier were both at the peak of their careers, and they went toe-to-toe for 14 rounds. The heat and humidity of Manila made the fight even more grueling, and both fighters were exhausted by the end of the fight. In the end, Ali emerged victorious, but both fighters earned each other's respect in a fight that will never be forgotten.

2. Sugar Ray Leonard vs. Thomas Hearns - "The Showdown"

This fight took place in 1981, and it was a battle between two of the greatest boxers of their generation, Sugar Ray Leonard and Thomas Hearns. Both fighters were undefeated at the time, and the anticipation for the fight was sky-high.

The fight lived up to the hype, as both fighters traded blows for 12 rounds. The fight was so close that it was difficult to determine who had won. In the end, Sugar Ray Leonard was declared the winner by a split decision, but the fight was so memorable that it is still talked about today.

3. Mike Tyson vs. Buster Douglas - "The Upset"

This fight took place in 1990, and it is one of the biggest upsets in boxing history. Mike Tyson was the heavyweight champion of the world and had never been defeated. Buster Douglas was considered a longshot to win the fight, but he shocked the world by knocking out Tyson in the 10th round.

The fight was a classic underdog story, as Douglas had overcome personal tragedy and adversity to earn his shot at the title. The fight is a reminder that anything can happen in boxing and that the underdog should never be underestimated.

4. Manny Pacquiao vs. Juan Manuel Marquez - "The Fourth Fight"

This fight took place in 2012, and it was the fourth fight between Manny Pacquiao and Juan Manuel Marquez. The previous three fights had all been close, with two of them ending in a draw.

The fourth fight was just as close, with both fighters trading blows for 12 rounds. In the end, Marquez landed a devastating right hand that knocked out Pacquiao in the sixth round. The fight was a reminder of the power of one punch and the importance of never letting your guard down in the ring.
