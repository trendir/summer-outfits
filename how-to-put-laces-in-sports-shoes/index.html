How To Put Laces In Sports Shoes A Beginner s Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your sports shoes coming untied during a game or workout? Are you unsure of how to properly lace your shoes for maximum comfort and support? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with putting laces in sports shoes, but with a little practice and patience, it can be a breeze. In this tutorial, we'll walk you through the steps of how to put laces in sports shoes, so you can step up your shoe game.

One of the biggest pain points people have when it comes to putting laces in sports shoes is finding the right lacing technique for their foot type and activity level. Some people have wide feet, high arches, or other foot conditions that require a specific lacing pattern. Additionally, different sports and activities may require different levels of support and tightness in the laces. All of these factors can make putting laces in sports shoes seem daunting and overwhelming.

First, start by loosening the laces on your sports shoes completely. This will make it easier to adjust the laces to fit your foot properly. Next, slide your foot into the shoe and pull the tongue up so it sits comfortably against your foot. Begin lacing up the shoe from the bottom, making sure to pull each lace tight as you go. As you move up the shoe, adjust the laces to fit your foot snugly but not too tight. Once you reach the top of the shoe, tie a knot to keep the laces in place.

In summary, when putting laces in sports shoes, it's important to find the right lacing technique for your foot type and activity level, loosen the laces completely, start at the bottom and work your way up, adjust the laces to fit snugly but not too tight, and tie a knot at the top to keep the laces in place.

How to Tie a Basic Knot

Before we dive deeper into lacing techniques, let's first cover how to tie a basic knot. Take one lace and make a loop, then cross the other lace over the loop and tuck it under. Pull both laces to tighten the knot. This knot is quick and easy to tie, but may come untied easily during activity.

Criss-Cross Lacing Pattern

The criss-cross lacing pattern is a classic and simple technique that works for most foot types and activities. Start by lacing up the bottom two eyelets straight across, then cross the laces over each other and go up to the next set of eyelets. Continue criss-crossing the laces up the shoe until you reach the top, then tie a knot as usual. This pattern provides even support and won't put too much pressure on any one part of the foot.

Loop-Back Lacing Pattern

The loop-back lacing pattern is a good option for people with high arches or wide feet. Start by lacing up the bottom two eyelets straight across, then take one lace and loop it back through the next eyelet on the same side. Repeat on the other side, then criss-cross the laces up the rest of the shoe as usual. This pattern provides extra support and room where it's needed most.

Straight-Bar Lacing Pattern

The straight-bar lacing pattern is a good option for people with narrow feet or who need extra support around the midfoot. Start by lacing up the bottom two eyelets straight across, then take one lace and go straight up to the next eyelet on the opposite side. Repeat on the other side, then criss-cross the laces up the rest of the shoe as usual. This pattern provides a secure fit and prevents slipping.

How to Double-Knot Your Laces

If you're tired of your laces coming untied during activity, try double-knotting them. After tying a basic knot, make another loop with each lace and tie them together in a bow. This will ensure that your laces stay in place no matter how hard you're working.

Question and Answer

Q: How tight should I tie my laces?

A: Your laces should be snug but not too tight. You should be able to wiggle your toes comfortably, but your foot should feel secure in the shoe.

Q: Can I use the same lacing pattern for all sports?

A: While some lacing patterns work for most activities, others may be better suited for specific sports or foot types. Experiment with different patterns to find what works best for you.

Q: What do I do if my laces are too long?

A: If your laces are too long, try tying a double knot or tucking the ends into the shoe. You can also replace your laces with shorter ones.

Q: How often should I replace my laces?

A: It's a good idea to replace your laces every six months to a year, depending on how often you wear your sports shoes and how hard you are on them.

Conclusion of How to Put Laces in Sports Shoes

Putting laces in sports shoes may seem like a small detail, but it can make a big difference in your comfort and performance. By finding the right lacing technique for your foot type and activity level, loosening the laces completely, starting at the bottom and working your way up, adjusting the laces to fit snugly but not too tight, and tying a knot at the top, you can ensure that your shoes feel comfortable and supportive during even the toughest workouts and games.
