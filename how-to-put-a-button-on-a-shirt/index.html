How To Put A Button On A Shirt A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 27, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you have a shirt with a missing button and don't know how to fix it? Are you struggling to put a button on a shirt without it falling off? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the process of putting a button on a shirt, so you can save money and avoid having to take it to a tailor.

Putting a button on a shirt may seem like a simple task, but it can be frustrating if you don't know the right technique. The button may fall off easily, or the thread may come loose, making the button insecure. These problems can be avoided by using the correct tools and following the steps correctly.

The first step to putting a button on a shirt is to choose the right button. Make sure it matches the other buttons on the shirt in terms of size, color, and style. You can find buttons at a fabric store or online, and they come in different shapes, sizes, and colors. Once you have the button, you will need a needle, thread, and a pair of scissors.

To begin, thread the needle with the thread and tie a knot at the end. Then, turn the shirt inside out and position the button where you want it to be. Make sure it is in the right place and mark the spots where the holes should be. You can use a fabric marker, chalk, or a pin to mark the spots.

Personal Experience:

When I first tried to put a button on a shirt, I didn't mark the spots where the holes should be, and the button ended up in the wrong place. I had to undo the stitches and start again, which was frustrating. Now, I always mark the spots before sewing the button.

Next, take the needle and thread and push it through the first hole from the inside of the shirt. Pull the thread all the way through until the knot is snug against the fabric. Then, push the needle through the corresponding hole on the button and pull the thread tight. Repeat this step a few times until the button is secure.

Personal Experience:

I used to sew the button on with just a couple of stitches, thinking it was enough. But then the button would fall off after a few wears. Now, I make sure to sew it on tightly, with several stitches to secure it.

After sewing the button securely, tie a knot at the back of the shirt and cut off any excess thread. Finally, turn the shirt right side out and admire your work! You have successfully put a button on a shirt.

Additional Tips:

When sewing the button, make sure it is not too tight or too loose. It should be secure but not too tight that it pulls the fabric. Also, if you are using a thicker fabric, you may need to use a longer needle to go through the fabric and button.

Using a Sewing Machine:

If you have a sewing machine, you can use it to sew on a button. Simply select the buttonhole stitch on the machine and position the shirt under the needle. The machine will sew the button and create a buttonhole automatically.

Personal Experience:

I have used a sewing machine to sew on buttons, and it is much quicker and easier than doing it by hand. However, I prefer to do it by hand because I feel like it is more secure and gives me more control.

Button on a Collar:

Putting a button on a collar is similar to putting a button on a shirt. You will need to choose the right button and position it correctly. However, you will need to use a thicker thread and make sure the button is secure enough to hold the collar in place.

Question and Answer:

Q: What kind of thread should I use to sew on a button?
A: You should use a high-quality thread that is strong and durable. Cotton or polyester thread is recommended, and make sure it matches the color of the fabric.

Q: How many stitches do I need to sew on a button?
A: You should sew on a button with at least four stitches, but you can add more if you want to make it more secure.

Q: Can I use glue to attach a button?
A: While glue may seem like a quick fix, it is not recommended for attaching buttons. It is not as secure as sewing and may come loose after a few washes.

Q: What if I don't have a needle or thread?
A: If you don't have a needle or thread, you can use a safety pin or a paper clip to attach the button temporarily. However, it is not a permanent solution, and you will need to sew on the button eventually.

Conclusion of How to Put a Button on a Shirt:

Putting a button on a shirt may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily. By following the steps outlined in this guide and using our additional tips, you can put a button on a shirt without any frustration. Now, you can save money and time by fixing your own shirts instead of taking them to a tailor.
