Question and Answer

Q. Can I use running shoes for other activities?

A. Running shoes are specifically designed for running, but they can also be used for other activities such as gym workouts and walking.

Q. How often should I replace my running shoes?

A. Running shoes should be replaced every 300-500 miles or every six months, whichever comes first.

Q. What is the difference between road running shoes and trail running shoes?

A. Road running shoes are designed for pavement and other hard surfaces, while trail running shoes are designed for uneven terrain and provide more traction and stability.

Q. How do I know if I overpronate or underpronate?

A. You can visit a specialty running store or consult with a podiatrist to determine your running gait and whether you overpronate, underpronate, or have a neutral gait.
