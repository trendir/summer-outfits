Discovering The Most Popular Ramen Brands In Korea A Guide

Written by Robby Sep 15, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a foodie looking to explore the world of instant noodles beyond your usual brands? Or perhaps you're a traveler seeking to immerse yourself in Korea's culinary scene? Either way, you can't go wrong with trying out the most popular ramen brands in Korea. Not only are they a staple in Korean households, but they also offer a diverse range of flavors and textures that cater to different tastes and preferences.

When it comes to Korean ramen, there are several factors to consider, such as the level of spiciness, the type of noodle, and the seasoning. Moreover, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. In this guide, we'll walk you through the most popular ramen brands in Korea and what makes them stand out.

If you're looking to try out the most popular ramen brands in Korea, you're in luck. These brands are widely available both in Korean supermarkets and online stores. Moreover, they're affordable and easy to prepare, making them a convenient option for busy days or lazy nights. Here are some of the most popular ramen brands in Korea:

1. Shin Ramyun

Shin Ramyun is undoubtedly one of the most iconic and recognizable ramen brands in Korea. It was first introduced in 1986 by Nongshim, a leading food manufacturer in Korea. Shin Ramyun is known for its spicy flavor and chewy noodles, which have a slightly thicker texture compared to other brands. It also comes with a seasoning packet that includes dried vegetables, such as shiitake mushrooms and green onions, which add depth and complexity to the broth.

2. Samyang Ramyun

If you're a fan of spicy food, you'll love Samyang Ramyun. This brand is famous for its "Buldak" (fire chicken) flavor, which is considered one of the spiciest ramen flavors in Korea. The noodles are thin and springy, and the broth has a rich and savory taste that complements the spiciness. Samyang Ramyun also offers other flavors, such as cheese, carbonara, and jjajang (black bean sauce).

3. Ottogi Jin Ramen

Ottogi Jin Ramen is a classic Korean ramen brand that has been around since 1984. It has a mild and savory taste that appeals to both adults and children. The noodles are thin and firm, and the broth has a subtle hint of beef and vegetables. Ottogi Jin Ramen also comes with a vegetable packet that includes dried carrots, green onions, and mushrooms.

4. Nongshim Chapagetti

Nongshim Chapagetti is a unique and popular ramen brand that combines the flavors of Korean jjajangmyeon (black bean noodles) and instant noodles. It has a sweet and savory taste that is derived from the black bean sauce, which is made from fermented soybeans. The noodles are thick and chewy, and the sauce has a thick and creamy consistency. Nongshim Chapagetti also comes with a vegetable packet that includes diced potatoes and onions.

Now that you know the most popular ramen brands in Korea, it's time to grab a bowl and dig in. Whether you prefer spicy or mild, thick or thin noodles, there's a Korean ramen brand that will satisfy your cravings. So go ahead and indulge in the flavors of Korea, one bowl at a time.

Personal Experience

As a Korean-American, I grew up eating Korean ramen and have tried various brands over the years. My personal favorite is Shin Ramyun, as I love the balance between the spiciness and the umami flavors of the broth. Whenever I'm craving comfort food, I reach for a bowl of Shin Ramyun and add an egg and some vegetables to make it more filling. It's the perfect meal for a lazy day or a quick lunch break.

How to Prepare Korean Ramen

Preparing Korean ramen is easy and straightforward. Here's a simple recipe that you can follow:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pack of Korean ramen
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 egg
  • Assorted vegetables (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a pot.
  2. Add the noodles and the seasoning packet to the pot.
  3. Let the noodles cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Meanwhile, fry an egg in a separate pan.
  5. Once the noodles are cooked, turn off the heat and transfer the noodles and the broth to a bowl.
  6. Add the fried egg and any vegetables you like (e.g., green onions, carrots, mushrooms).
  7. Enjoy!

Q&A

Q: Are Korean ramen brands vegan-friendly?

A: It depends on the brand and the flavor. Some Korean ramen brands, such as Ottogi Jin Ramen and Nongshim Chapagetti, have vegan-friendly options that don't contain any animal products. However, other brands, such as Samyang Ramyun, may contain meat or dairy ingredients. It's always best to check the ingredients list before purchasing.

Q: Can I add other ingredients to Korean ramen?

A: Yes! One of the great things about Korean ramen is that you can customize it to your liking. You can add vegetables, such as carrots, cabbage, or mushrooms, to make it more nutritious. You can also add proteins, such as eggs, tofu, or meat, to make it more filling. The possibilities are endless!

Q: Can I eat Korean ramen as a meal?

A: Yes, you can! Korean ramen is a convenient and satisfying meal that you can enjoy any time of the day. However, keep in mind that it's high in sodium and calories, so it's best to consume it in moderation and balance it with other nutritious foods.

Q: Where can I buy Korean ramen?

A: Korean ramen brands are widely available in Korean supermarkets and Asian grocery stores. You can also buy them online from websites such as Amazon or Hmart.

Conclusion of Most Popular Ramen Brands in Korea

In conclusion, Korean ramen brands offer a delicious and convenient way to explore Korean cuisine. From the iconic spiciness of Shin Ramyun to the sweet and savory taste of Nongshim Chapagetti, there's a brand and a flavor for everyone. So next time you're in the mood for a quick and satisfying meal, grab a bowl of Korean ramen and indulge in the flavors of Korea.
