What Can You Expect As A Product Designer Salary In India

Written by Luffy Oct 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Product design is an exciting and rewarding career field that allows individuals to be creative while solving problems. However, one of the most common concerns for those pursuing a career in product design is what the salary expectations are in India. As with any industry, there are factors that can influence a product designer salary in India, such as experience, location, and industry.

When it comes to product designer salary in India, the target can vary depending on a variety of factors. In general, a product designer in India can expect to make an average salary of around INR 600,000 per year. However, this number can vary significantly based on the individual's experience, the company they work for, and the region they work in.

To summarize, product designer salary in India is dependent on various factors. While the average salary for a product designer in India is around INR 600,000 per year, this number can vary based on individual factors such as experience, location, and industry.

Factors Influencing Product Designer Salary in India

There are several factors that can influence a product designer salary in India. Below are two examples of how location and experience can impact the salary of a product designer.

Location

The location that a product designer works in can have a significant impact on their salary. For example, a product designer working in a major city such as Delhi or Mumbai may earn a higher salary than someone working in a smaller city or rural area. This is because the cost of living is generally higher in major cities, and companies may need to offer higher salaries to attract top talent.

Experience

Experience is another factor that can impact a product designer salary in India. As with many industries, those with more experience generally earn higher salaries. A product designer who has been working in the industry for several years will likely earn a higher salary than someone who is just starting out.

How to Negotiate Your Product Designer Salary in India

When it comes to negotiating your product designer salary in India, it's important to do your research and know your worth. Consider factors such as your experience, skills, and the industry standards for your location. Additionally, be prepared to have an open and honest conversation with your employer about your expectations and what you bring to the table. Remember that negotiation is a two-way street, and both parties should feel satisfied with the final agreement.

Personal Experience with Product Designer Salary in India

As a product designer in India with five years of experience, I have found that salaries can vary significantly depending on the company and location. I have worked for both start-ups and established companies, and have found that start-ups typically offer lower salaries but more opportunities for growth and creativity. On the other hand, established companies may offer higher salaries but can be more rigid in their design processes.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for a product designer in India?

A: The average salary for a product designer in India is around INR 600,000 per year.

Q: Can experience impact a product designer's salary in India?

A: Yes, experience is one of the factors that can impact a product designer salary in India. Those with more experience generally earn higher salaries.

Q: How can I negotiate my product designer salary in India?

A: Do your research, know your worth, and have an open and honest conversation with your employer about your expectations.

Q: What are some factors that can influence a product designer salary in India?

A: Location, experience, and industry are all factors that can impact a product designer salary in India.

Conclusion of Product Designer Salary in India

Product design is an exciting and lucrative career field in India. While the average salary for a product designer in India is around INR 600,000 per year, this number can vary based on individual factors such as experience, location, and industry. By doing your research, knowing your worth, and having an open and honest conversation with your employer, you can negotiate a salary that reflects your skills and experience.
