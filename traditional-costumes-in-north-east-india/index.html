Traditional Costumes In North East India A Guide To The Region s Rich Cultural Heritage

Written by William Aug 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're interested in learning more about the traditional costumes of North East India, you've come to the right place. This region is home to a diverse range of cultures, each with their own unique clothing traditions. From the vibrant colors of Assam's mekhela chador to the intricate embroidery of Nagaland's shawls, there's a lot to discover.

For many people in North East India, traditional costumes are much more than just a fashion statement. They're a way of expressing cultural identity and heritage, and are often deeply intertwined with rituals and ceremonies. However, in recent years, there has been concern about the decline of traditional clothing practices as younger generations opt for more modern styles.

The target of traditional costumes in North East India is to promote and preserve the region's rich cultural heritage through the continuation of traditional clothing practices.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at some of the key traditional costumes of North East India and their significance. We'll also explore the challenges facing the preservation of traditional clothing practices and the efforts being made to overcome them.

The Mekhela Chador of Assam

As one of the most recognizable traditional costumes of North East India, the mekhela chador is a two-piece garment worn by women in Assam. The bottom piece, called the mekhela, is a long skirt that is draped around the waist and tucked in at the back. The top piece, known as the chador, is a long piece of cloth that is draped over the head and shoulders.

When I visited Assam last year, I was struck by the vibrant colors and intricate designs of the mekhela chador. Each piece is made by hand, with skilled weavers using techniques that have been passed down through generations. The mekhela chador is often worn for special occasions such as weddings and festivals, and is a symbol of Assamese pride and identity.

The Shawls of Nagaland

Another key traditional costume of North East India is the shawl of Nagaland. These intricately woven garments are often made from locally sourced materials such as cotton, wool, and silk. They are known for their vibrant colors and intricate patterns, which often depict animals and other elements of nature.

During my travels in Nagaland, I had the opportunity to witness a shawl-making demonstration by a local weaver. It was fascinating to see the level of skill and attention to detail that goes into creating these beautiful garments. However, despite their importance to the cultural identity of the region, the production of traditional shawls is facing a number of challenges.

The Importance of Preservation

As with many traditional clothing practices around the world, the production of traditional costumes in North East India is at risk of decline. Younger generations are often more interested in modern clothing styles, and the skills and knowledge required to create traditional garments are in danger of being lost.

However, there are efforts being made to preserve these important cultural traditions. Organizations such as the North East Zone Cultural Centre are working to promote awareness and appreciation of traditional clothing practices, and to provide support and resources to artisans and weavers.

How to Support Traditional Clothing Practices

If you're interested in supporting the preservation of traditional clothing practices in North East India, there are a number of things you can do. One of the most important is to educate yourself about the cultural significance of these garments and the challenges facing their production.

You can also support local artisans and weavers by purchasing traditional garments and accessories. This helps to provide a livelihood for those who are keeping these important cultural traditions alive.

The Future of Traditional Costumes in North East India

While the challenges facing the preservation of traditional clothing practices in North East India are significant, there is hope for the future. Through education, awareness, and support, it's possible to ensure that these important cultural traditions continue to thrive for generations to come.
