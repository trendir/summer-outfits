Is Sherwani National Dress Of Pakistan

Written by Christine Jul 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

When it comes to traditional clothing, every country has its unique style that represents its culture and heritage. In the case of Pakistan, Sherwani is one of the most popular traditional attires. However, many people wonder if Sherwani is the national dress of Pakistan.

The confusion about the national dress of Pakistan is quite common, especially among foreigners or those who are not familiar with the country's culture. It is essential to understand the significance of traditional clothing and its historical background before identifying the national dress of Pakistan.

Sherwani is not the national dress of Pakistan. Pakistan does not have an officially declared national dress. However, Sherwani is considered one of the most popular traditional outfits, especially for men, and is widely worn on formal occasions such as weddings, festivals, and other celebrations.

In summary, Sherwani is not the national dress of Pakistan, but it is a cultural symbol that reflects the rich heritage and traditions of the country.

The Significance of Sherwani in Pakistani Culture

As mentioned earlier, Sherwani is a traditional attire that is widely popular in Pakistan, especially among men. It is a long coat-like garment that is worn over a Kurta and Churidar or Shalwar. Sherwani is typically made of silk or cotton and adorned with intricate embroidery, beads, and other embellishments.

Personally, I have always found Sherwani to be a unique and elegant dress. I have worn it on several occasions, including my brother's wedding, and it always makes me feel confident and proud of my cultural heritage.

Sherwani has a significant cultural and historical background. It traces its roots back to the Mughal era when it was worn by the nobility and the royals. Sherwani was considered a symbol of status and prestige and was often adorned with precious jewels and gems.

Over time, Sherwani became a popular attire among the masses, and it evolved into a more practical and accessible form. Today, it is an essential part of Pakistani culture and is worn on special occasions such as weddings, Eid, and other festivities.

How to Wear Sherwani?

Wearing a Sherwani is not as complicated as it may seem. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wear Sherwani:

Step 1:

Wear a Kurta and Churidar or Shalwar as a base.

Step 2:

Put on the Sherwani over the Kurta and Churidar or Shalwar.

Step 3:

Button up the Sherwani from top to bottom.

Step 4:

Adjust the collar and cuffs of the Sherwani to fit properly.

Step 5:

Complete the look with a pair of traditional shoes, such as Khussa or Peshawari Chappal.

FAQs

Q: Is Sherwani only for Men?

A: Yes, Sherwani is typically worn by men in Pakistan.

Q: What is the difference between Sherwani and Achkan?

A: Sherwani and Achkan are both long coats worn over a Kurta and Churidar or Shalwar. However, Sherwani is more formal and ornate, while Achkan is simpler and more practical.

Q: What are the different types of Sherwani?

A: There are several types of Sherwani, including Achkan Sherwani, Jodhpuri Sherwani, Angrakha Sherwani, and Indo-Western Sherwani.

Q: Can I wear Sherwani on any occasion?

A: Sherwani is typically worn on formal occasions such as weddings, Eid, and other celebrations.

Conclusion

Although Sherwani is not the national dress of Pakistan, it is undoubtedly one of the most significant cultural symbols that represent the country's rich heritage and traditions. It is a unique and elegant attire that is widely popular among men in Pakistan and is worn on special occasions such as weddings, Eid, and other festivities.
