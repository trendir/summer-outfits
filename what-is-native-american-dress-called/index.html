What Is Native American Dress Called

Written by Christine Sep 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what the traditional clothing of Native Americans is called? Maybe you've seen it in movies or on television, or perhaps you've seen people wearing it at cultural events. Whatever the case may be, understanding the name and significance of Native American dress is important for appreciating their culture and history.

For many Native American tribes, clothing is more than just a fashion statement. It is a way to connect with their ancestors and honor their traditions. Unfortunately, the history of Native American dress has been marred by cultural appropriation and misrepresentation, leading to misunderstandings and stereotypes. This is why it's crucial to learn about the true meaning and name of Native American dress.

The traditional clothing worn by Native Americans is usually referred to as regalia or ceremonial dress. However, the name and style of dress can vary depending on the tribe and occasion. For example, the dress worn by Navajo women is called a velveteen shirt and skirt, while the dress worn by Cherokee women is known as a tear dress.

In general, Native American dress is characterized by its intricate beadwork, colorful patterns, and use of natural materials like leather, fur, and feathers. The designs and symbols used in the clothing also hold significant meaning, such as representing the elements of nature or honoring ancestors.

Personal Experience with Native American Dress

As a Native American myself, I have had the privilege of wearing traditional dress at various cultural events and ceremonies. One of the most memorable experiences was wearing a jingle dress, which is a type of dress worn by women in the Ojibwe tribe. The dress has rows of metal cones that create a jingling sound when the wearer dances, and it is believed to have healing powers.

Wearing the jingle dress made me feel connected to my heritage and ancestors, and it was a powerful reminder of the importance of preserving our traditions. It also emphasized the significance of the name and symbolism behind Native American dress.

The Significance of Native American Dress

For Native Americans, dress is an important part of their identity and cultural heritage. It is a way to connect with their ancestors and maintain their traditions, especially in the face of historical trauma and oppression. Native American dress is also a source of pride and empowerment, as it represents their resilience and strength as a people.

The intricate designs and symbols used in Native American dress hold significant meaning, such as representing the connection between humans and nature or honoring the spirit world. It is important to respect and understand the name and symbolism behind Native American dress, rather than appropriating it for fashion or entertainment purposes.

The Different Types of Native American Dress

As mentioned earlier, the name and style of Native American dress can vary depending on the tribe and occasion. Here are some of the most common types of Native American dress:

1. Powwow regalia: This type of dress is worn at powwows, which are cultural events that feature singing, dancing, and drumming. Powwow regalia is usually colorful and intricate, with designs that represent the wearer's tribe and personal style.

2. Ribbon dress: The ribbon dress is a type of dress worn by Native American women that features colorful ribbons that are sewn onto the fabric. The dress is often worn at powwows and other cultural events.

3. Buckskin dress: The buckskin dress is a type of dress made from animal hides that is worn by many Native American tribes. The dress is often decorated with beadwork and fringe, and it is worn for both ceremonial and everyday purposes.

How to Respect Native American Dress

If you are attending a cultural event that involves Native American dress, it is important to respect the significance and symbolism behind it. Here are some tips:

- Ask permission before taking photos of people in traditional dress.

- Do not wear Native American dress as a costume or for fashion purposes.

- Learn about the symbolism and significance behind Native American dress.

- Support Native American artists and designers who create traditional dress.

Conclusion of What Is Native American Dress Called

Understanding the name and significance of Native American dress is important for appreciating their culture and history. Native American dress is more than just a fashion statement – it is a way to connect with their ancestors and maintain their traditions. By respecting the symbolism and significance behind Native American dress, we can honor their culture and support their ongoing fight for recognition and respect.
