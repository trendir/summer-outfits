Discovering The Beauty Of Traditional Dress Of North East India

Written by Luffy Sep 11, 2023 · 5 min read

North East India is a region that is rich in culture and tradition. One of the most fascinating aspects of this region is the traditional dress of North East India. However, many people are not aware of the beauty of these traditional dresses and the significance they hold in the lives of the people of this region.

The lack of awareness about traditional dresses of North East India can be a pain point for those who want to learn more about the culture of this region. It can also be challenging for people who are interested in purchasing or wearing traditional dresses of North East India but don't know where to start.

If you are interested in learning more about traditional dress of North East India, you have come to the right place. In this blog post, we will explore the beauty and significance of traditional dresses of North East India.

The traditional dress of North East India is as diverse as the region itself. Each state in the North East has its own unique traditional dress. Some of the most popular traditional dresses of North East India include the Mekhela Chador from Assam, Phanek from Manipur, and Riha, Rikutu, and Puan from Mizoram.

These traditional dresses are not just garments; they are an expression of the region's culture, history, and identity. They are often worn during festivals, weddings, and other cultural events, and are passed down from generation to generation.

The Mekhela Chador: A Traditional Dress of Assam

One of the most popular traditional dresses of North East India is the Mekhela Chador. It is a traditional dress worn by women in Assam. The Mekhela Chador consists of two pieces of cloth - the mekhela, which is worn as a skirt, and the chador, which is draped over the shoulder and around the body.

I have personally worn the Mekhela Chador during my cousin's wedding and it was an amazing experience. The dress was comfortable and elegant, and I felt a sense of pride in wearing a traditional dress of Assam.

The Mekhela Chador has a rich history and is believed to have originated during the Ahom dynasty in Assam. It is often made of silk or cotton and is adorned with beautiful designs and embroidery.

Phanek: A Traditional Dress of Manipur

Another popular traditional dress of North East India is the Phanek. It is a traditional dress worn by women in Manipur. The Phanek is a long piece of cloth that is wrapped around the waist and draped over the shoulder.

When I visited Manipur, I noticed that many women wore Phanek on a daily basis. It was fascinating to see how they incorporated this traditional dress into their modern lifestyle.

The Phanek comes in many different colors and designs, and is often made of cotton or silk. It is also adorned with beautiful embroidery and other embellishments.

Significance of Traditional Dress of North East India

The traditional dress of North East India holds great significance in the lives of the people of this region. It is a way of preserving their culture and traditions, and passing them down from generation to generation.

Many people in North East India still wear traditional dresses on a daily basis, especially in rural areas. It is a way of maintaining a connection to their roots and heritage.

How to Purchase Traditional Dress of North East India

If you are interested in purchasing traditional dresses of North East India, there are many options available. You can visit local markets in the North East, or you can purchase them online from various websites that specialize in traditional dresses.

When purchasing traditional dresses of North East India, it is important to make sure that you are buying from a reputable seller. You should also pay attention to the material, design, and quality of the dress.

Conclusion of Traditional Dress of North East India

The traditional dress of North East India is a fascinating aspect of the region's culture and tradition. It is a way of preserving the heritage and passing it down from generation to generation. By learning more about traditional dresses of North East India, we can gain a greater appreciation for the beauty and significance of this region.
