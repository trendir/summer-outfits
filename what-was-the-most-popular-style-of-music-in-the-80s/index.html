The Most Popular Style Of Music In The 80S A Tutorial

Written by Oliver Sep 05, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're a fan of 80s music, you may be wondering what made it so popular. The 80s was a decade of cultural and technological change, and the music of the time reflected these changes. However, not all music styles were equally popular in the 80s, and some were more popular than others. In this tutorial, we will explore what was the most popular style of music in the 80s and why.

The 80s was a time of great social and political upheaval, and people were looking for music that reflected their feelings and experiences. They wanted music that was upbeat, energetic, and optimistic, but also music that was rebellious and anti-establishment. This created a need for a new kind of music that was different from the 70s music that had dominated the previous decade.

The most popular style of music in the 80s was undoubtedly pop music. Pop music was the perfect fit for the 80s because it was upbeat, energetic, and had a positive message. It was also heavily influenced by the new technology of the time, such as synthesizers and drum machines, which gave it a futuristic sound. Pop music was everywhere in the 80s, and it dominated the airwaves, the charts, and the music videos.

In summary, the most popular style of music in the 80s was pop music. This was due to its upbeat and energetic nature, its positive message, and its heavy use of new technology. Pop music was everywhere in the 80s, and it dominated the music industry.

Pop Music in the 80s: A Personal Experience

As a child of the 80s, I have fond memories of listening to pop music on the radio and watching music videos on MTV. I remember dancing to songs like "Wake Me Up Before You Go-Go" by Wham! and "Girls Just Want to Have Fun" by Cyndi Lauper. These songs were so catchy and fun, and they always put me in a good mood.

Pop music in the 80s was more than just catchy tunes, though. It was also a reflection of the cultural and technological changes of the time. The use of synthesizers and drum machines gave pop music a futuristic sound that was unlike anything that had come before. This made it exciting and innovative, and it captured the spirit of the 80s perfectly.

The Evolution of Pop Music in the 80s

Pop music in the 80s was not a static genre. It evolved and changed throughout the decade, reflecting the changing tastes of the audience and the influence of new artists. In the early 80s, pop music was heavily influenced by disco and funk, with artists like Michael Jackson and Prince dominating the charts.

However, as the decade progressed, pop music began to incorporate more elements of rock music, with bands like Duran Duran and Bon Jovi becoming popular. This created a new sub-genre of pop music called "pop rock", which combined the catchy melodies of pop with the guitar-driven sound of rock.

The Legacy of Pop Music in the 80s

Pop music in the 80s had a profound influence on the music industry and the culture as a whole. It introduced new technologies and sounds that would shape the future of music, and it inspired countless artists to create their own unique styles. It also had a positive message that resonated with audiences around the world, promoting unity, love, and acceptance.

How to Appreciate Pop Music in the 80s

If you want to appreciate pop music in the 80s, there are a few things you can do. First, listen to some of the classic songs from the era, such as "Billie Jean" by Michael Jackson or "Like a Virgin" by Madonna. Second, watch some of the iconic music videos from the time, such as "Thriller" by Michael Jackson or "Material Girl" by Madonna. Finally, read some articles or books about the history of pop music in the 80s to gain a deeper understanding of the genre.

Conclusion of What Was the Most Popular Style of Music in the 80s

In conclusion, pop music was the most popular style of music in the 80s. Its upbeat and energetic nature, its positive message, and its heavy use of new technology made it the perfect fit for the cultural and technological changes of the time. Pop music was everywhere in the 80s, and it had a profound influence on the music industry and the culture as a whole.
