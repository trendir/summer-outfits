Types Of Indian Bridal Wear A Comprehensive Guide

Indian weddings are known for their grandeur and extravagance, and the bride's outfit is one of the most important aspects of the ceremony. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the perfect bridal wear. In this guide, we will explore the different types of Indian bridal wear and help you find the one that fits your style and personality.

Choosing the right bridal wear can be a daunting task, especially when you don't know where to start. It's important to consider factors such as the bride's body type, the wedding theme, and the cultural background. Additionally, the bride may have her own preferences and style that she wants to incorporate into her outfit.

The target of this article is to provide a comprehensive guide to the different types of Indian bridal wear, including sarees, lehengas, salwar kameez, and more. We will also explore the different fabrics, colors, and embellishments that are commonly used in Indian bridal wear. By the end of this guide, you will have a better understanding of the different types of Indian bridal wear and be able to make an informed decision on your wedding outfit.

In summary, Indian bridal wear is an important aspect of the wedding ceremony, and choosing the right outfit can be overwhelming. This guide will provide a comprehensive overview of the different types of Indian bridal wear, including sarees, lehengas, salwar kameez, and more, as well as the different fabrics, colors, and embellishments that are commonly used.

Sarees

Sarees are one of the most traditional forms of Indian bridal wear and are still popular today. They are made from a single piece of fabric that is draped around the body and over the shoulder. Sarees come in a variety of fabrics such as silk, chiffon, georgette, and cotton, and are available in a range of colors and designs.

When I got married, I chose to wear a red silk saree with intricate gold embroidery. The color red is considered auspicious in Indian culture and is a popular choice for brides. The saree was lightweight and comfortable, which was important as I had to wear it for several hours. The gold embroidery added an elegant touch to the outfit and complemented my jewelry.

Lehengas

Lehengas are a popular choice for Indian brides and are a type of long skirt that is paired with a blouse and dupatta (a long scarf). They come in a variety of fabrics such as silk, velvet, and net, and are available in a range of colors and designs.

For my sister's wedding, she chose to wear a heavily embellished lehenga in a deep maroon color. The lehenga had intricate embroidery and beadwork, which added a touch of glamour to the outfit. The dupatta was draped over her head and completed the look.

Salwar Kameez

Salwar kameez is a type of Indian bridal wear that consists of a long tunic (kameez) paired with loose-fitting pants (salwar) and a scarf (dupatta). They come in a variety of fabrics such as cotton, silk, and chiffon, and are available in a range of colors and designs.

My friend chose to wear a pastel-colored salwar kameez for her wedding ceremony. The outfit was simple yet elegant, and the color complemented her complexion. The dupatta was draped over her head and added a traditional touch to the outfit.

Anarkalis

Anarkalis are a type of Indian bridal wear that consists of a long, flowing tunic paired with fitted pants. They come in a variety of fabrics such as silk, georgette, and chiffon, and are available in a range of colors and designs. Anarkalis are a popular choice for brides who want a comfortable yet stylish outfit.

For my cousin's wedding, she chose to wear a heavily embellished anarkali in a deep blue color. The outfit had intricate embroidery and sequin work, which added a touch of glamour to the outfit. The fitted pants were comfortable to wear and allowed her to move around easily during the ceremony.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the most popular color for Indian bridal wear?

A: Red is considered the most auspicious color for Indian brides, but other popular colors include gold, maroon, and pink.

Q: What kind of jewelry is typically worn with Indian bridal wear?

A: Indian brides typically wear heavy gold jewelry, including necklaces, bangles, earrings, and nose rings. The jewelry is often studded with precious stones and adds a touch of glamour to the outfit.

Q: Can Indian bridal wear be customized?

A: Yes, many designers offer customized Indian bridal wear that is tailored to the bride's preferences and style. This allows the bride to create a unique outfit that reflects her personality.

Q: How long does it take to purchase Indian bridal wear?

A: Purchasing Indian bridal wear can take several weeks or even months, depending on the complexity of the outfit and the availability of the fabric and embellishments. It's important to start the shopping process early to ensure that the outfit is ready in time for the wedding.

Conclusion of Types of Indian Bridal Wear

Choosing the perfect Indian bridal wear can be overwhelming, but by understanding the different types of outfits available, brides can make an informed decision that reflects their style and personality. Whether you choose a saree, lehenga, salwar kameez, or anarkali, make sure to consider factors such as the fabric, color, and embellishments. By finding the perfect outfit, brides can feel confident and beautiful on their special day.
