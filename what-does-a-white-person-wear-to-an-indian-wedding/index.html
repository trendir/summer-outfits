What Does A White Person Wear To An Indian Wedding

Written by Aprilia Jun 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Indian weddings are known for their grandeur, lavishness, and colorful celebrations. However, for a white person attending an Indian wedding, the dress code can be a bit confusing. You want to dress appropriately for the occasion while still being comfortable and respectful of the culture. In this blog post, we will go over what a white person should wear to an Indian wedding and provide tips to make the process easier.

Attending an Indian wedding as a white person can be intimidating. You want to make sure you are not underdressed or overdressed. You also want to make sure you are respectful of the culture and traditions. It can be challenging to navigate what to wear in a different cultural setting.

When attending an Indian wedding as a white person, it is best to dress in traditional Indian attire. Men can wear a kurta pyjama, which is a long shirt and pants with a scarf. Women can wear a saree, lehenga, or salwar kameez. These are all traditional Indian outfits that are comfortable and respectful of the culture. If you are not comfortable wearing traditional Indian attire, you can opt for western formal wear such as a suit or a dress.

In summary, the best attire for a white person attending an Indian wedding is traditional Indian attire such as a kurta pyjama for men and a saree, lehenga, or salwar kameez for women. If you are not comfortable wearing traditional Indian attire, western formal wear such as a suit or a dress is also acceptable.

Personal Experience with What Does a White Person Wear to an Indian Wedding

As a white person attending an Indian wedding, I was nervous about what to wear. I decided to wear a saree, which was comfortable and respectful of the culture. I received many compliments on my outfit and felt like I fit in with the rest of the guests. It was a great experience to embrace the culture and traditions of the wedding.

Tips for What Does a White Person Wear to an Indian Wedding

1. Research the dress code beforehand.

2. Opt for traditional Indian attire if possible.

3. If you are not comfortable wearing traditional Indian attire, western formal wear such as a suit or a dress is also acceptable.

4. Be respectful of the culture and traditions.

Why Wear Traditional Indian Attire?

Wearing traditional Indian attire shows respect for the culture and traditions of the wedding. It also allows you to fully embrace the experience and feel like a part of the celebration.

How to Choose the Right Traditional Indian Attire?

Choosing the right traditional Indian attire can be overwhelming. Consider the dress code, the time of day, and the formality of the wedding. It is also helpful to ask the bride or groom for suggestions on what to wear.

Personal Experience with Choosing Traditional Indian Attire

When choosing my traditional Indian attire for an Indian wedding, I consulted with the bride for suggestions. She recommended a saree in a specific color scheme. I was able to find a beautiful saree that fit the dress code and made me feel comfortable and respectful of the culture.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it appropriate to wear jeans to an Indian wedding?

A: No, jeans are not appropriate for an Indian wedding.

Q: Can I wear black to an Indian wedding?

A: It is best to avoid black as it is traditionally associated with mourning in Indian culture.

Q: Can I wear open-toed shoes to an Indian wedding?

A: Yes, open-toed shoes are acceptable as long as they are dressy and complement the outfit.

Q: Is it appropriate to wear a hat to an Indian wedding?

A: No, hats are not appropriate for an Indian wedding.

Conclusion of What Does a White Person Wear to an Indian Wedding

Attending an Indian wedding as a white person can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. By following these tips, you can dress appropriately for the occasion while still being comfortable and respectful of the culture. Remember to research the dress code beforehand, opt for traditional Indian attire if possible, and be respectful of the culture and traditions.
