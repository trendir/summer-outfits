Discover Traditional Italian Easter Dessert Recipes

Written by Eveline Nov 11, 2023 쨌 5 min read

Are you looking to impress your guests with an authentic Italian Easter dessert spread? Look no further than these traditional Italian Easter dessert recipes! From sweet and fruity to rich and chocolatey, there is something for every palate.

Italian Easter desserts are an essential part of the holiday feast, and they are steeped in history and tradition. These recipes have been passed down from generation to generation, and they are still enjoyed by Italians all over the world today.

In this article, we will explore some of the most beloved traditional Italian Easter dessert recipes. From the classic Colomba di Pasqua to the rich and decadent Torta Pasqualina, we will cover everything you need to know to make an authentic Italian Easter dessert spread.

Let's dive in and discover the delicious world of traditional Italian Easter desserts!

Pastiera Napoletana

One of the most famous traditional Italian Easter desserts is the Pastiera Napoletana. This sweet and creamy dessert hails from Naples and is made with ricotta cheese, wheat berries, and candied fruit.

I remember my grandmother making this dessert every Easter, and the smell of it baking in the oven filled the entire house. It was always a family favorite, and we would eagerly anticipate digging into it after our Easter feast.

To make Pastiera Napoletana, you will need:

Ingredients:

  • 500g of cooked wheat berries
  • 500g of ricotta cheese
  • 300g of sugar
  • 6 eggs
  • 100g of candied fruit
  • 1 lemon zest
  • 1 orange zest
  • 1 vanilla pod
  • 50g of butter
  • 1 tbsp of flour
  • 1 tbsp of milk
  • 1 pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180째C.
  2. In a mixing bowl, beat the eggs with the sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the ricotta cheese, wheat berries, candied fruit, lemon zest, orange zest, and vanilla pod. Mix well.
  4. Grease a 10-inch cake tin with butter and dust with flour.
  5. Pour the mixture into the tin.
  6. Bake for 1 hour or until golden brown.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool.
  8. Once cool, dust with powdered sugar and serve.

Colomba di Pasqua

Another classic Italian Easter dessert is the Colomba di Pasqua. This dove-shaped cake is similar to the famous Italian Christmas cake, Panettone, and is traditionally made with candied orange peel and almonds.

My family always served Colomba di Pasqua alongside Pastiera Napoletana, and it was the perfect complement to the rich and creamy dessert. It's also a great option for those who prefer a lighter dessert.

To make Colomba di Pasqua, you will need:

Ingredients:

  • 500g of flour
  • 150g of sugar
  • 150g of butter
  • 100ml of milk
  • 3 eggs
  • 20g of yeast
  • 100g of candied orange peel
  • 100g of almonds
  • 1 pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, mix the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
  2. Add the eggs, butter, and milk. Mix well.
  3. Add the candied orange peel and almonds. Mix well.
  4. Grease a dove-shaped cake tin with butter and dust with flour.
  5. Pour the mixture into the tin.
  6. Bake for 45 minutes or until golden brown.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool.
  8. Once cool, dust with powdered sugar and serve.

Torta Pasqualina

The Torta Pasqualina is a savory Italian Easter pie that is traditionally made with spinach, ricotta cheese, and eggs. It's a great option for those who prefer a less sweet dessert.

My family always served Torta Pasqualina alongside the sweet desserts, and it was a nice break from all the sugar. It's also a great option for a light lunch or dinner during the Easter holiday.

To make Torta Pasqualina, you will need:

Ingredients:

  • 500g of spinach
  • 500g of ricotta cheese
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 onion
  • 100g of parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of butter
  • 1 tbsp of flour
  • 1 pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180째C.
  2. Wash the spinach and cook in a frying pan with a little olive oil until wilted. Drain well and let cool.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat the eggs with the ricotta cheese and parmesan cheese.
  4. Add the spinach and mix well. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  5. Grease a 10-inch cake tin with butter and dust with flour.
  6. Pour the mixture into the tin.
  7. Bake for 1 hour or until golden brown.
  8. Remove from the oven and let cool.
  9. Once cool, slice and serve.

Questions and Answers

Q: What is the history behind Italian Easter desserts?

A: Italian Easter desserts have a long history and are steeped in tradition. Many of the recipes date back to ancient times and have been passed down from generation to generation.

Q: Are there any traditional Italian Easter desserts that are gluten-free?

A: Yes, there are several traditional Italian Easter desserts that are gluten-free, such as the Pastiera Napoletana made with almond flour instead of wheat flour.

Q: Can I make these desserts ahead of time?

A: Yes, all of these desserts can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to three days.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to make these desserts?

A: No, all of these desserts can be made with basic kitchen equipment such as mixing bowls, cake tins, and frying pans.

Conclusion of Traditional Italian Easter Dessert Recipes

Italian Easter desserts are a delicious and essential part of the holiday feast. From the sweet and creamy Pastiera Napoletana to the savory Torta Pasqualina, there is something for every palate. These traditional Italian Easter dessert recipes have been passed down from generation to generation and are still enjoyed by Italians all over the world today. So why not add some Italian flair to your Easter celebration this year and try making one of these delicious desserts?
