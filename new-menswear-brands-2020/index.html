Finding Your Style with New Menswear Brands 2020

One of the great things about new menswear brands 2020 is the range of styles and designs they offer. Whether you're looking for classic, timeless pieces or bold, modern styles, there's a brand out there that's perfect for you.

For example, if you're looking for casual wear that's both comfortable and stylish, you might check out brands like Outerknown, Marine Layer, or Faherty. These brands offer a range of casual wear, from t-shirts and shorts to jeans and jackets, all made with high-quality materials and designed with a laid-back, California vibe.

If you're more interested in formal wear, you might check out brands like Suitsupply, Oliver Wicks, or Black Lapel. These brands offer made-to-measure suits and other formal wear that's both stylish and affordable, with a range of fabrics, colors, and styles to choose from.

One of the biggest trends this year is the return of the 90s. From oversized shirts and baggy jeans to chunky sneakers and bucket hats, the 90s are back in a big way. Another trend we're seeing is the rise of bold prints and colors, from bright florals and geometric patterns to neon hues and pastel shades.

Other trends this year include classic cuts and silhouettes, like the double-breasted blazer and the wide-leg trouser. We're also seeing a return to more traditional materials and fabrics, like wool, tweed, and corduroy, as well as a focus on sustainable and eco-friendly materials.

Finding the Best Deals on New Menswear Brands 2020

Of course, staying on-trend can be expensive, especially when it comes to new menswear brands 2020. But there are ways to find the latest styles without breaking the bank.

One tip is to shop off-season. Many brands offer deep discounts on their off-season styles, so if you're willing to wait a bit, you can save a lot of money. Another tip is to sign up for email newsletters and follow brands on social media, as they often offer exclusive discounts and deals to their fans and followers.

You can also check out online marketplaces like Grailed and Depop, where you can find gently-used and second-hand styles at a fraction of the retail price. And don't forget about thrift stores and vintage shops, where you can often find unique and one-of-a-kind pieces that are both stylish and affordable.
