Traditional Indian Clothing For Male Weddings

Written by Daniel Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to weddings, traditional Indian clothing for men can often be a source of confusion and frustration. From the different types of garments to the intricate designs and colors, it can be overwhelming to navigate the world of Indian fashion. However, dressing in traditional attire is an important part of Indian culture and represents respect for the occasion.

For men attending an Indian wedding, the traditional attire consists of a kurta, pajama, and a dupatta (optional). The kurta is a long shirt that falls below the knees and can be made of silk, cotton, or other fabrics. The pajama is a loose-fitting trouser that is usually made of cotton or silk. The dupatta is a long scarf that can be draped over the shoulder or left hanging.

To complete the traditional look, men can also wear a jacket over the kurta, called a Nehru jacket. This jacket is named after Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, who often wore this style of jacket. The Nehru jacket is a fitted jacket with a Mandarin collar and can be made of silk, cotton, or other fabrics. It is often embellished with embroidery or other types of embellishments.

In summary, the traditional Indian clothing for male weddings consists of a kurta, pajama, and optional dupatta, with a Nehru jacket to complete the look.

Choosing the Right Attire

When it comes to choosing the right attire for an Indian wedding, it is important to consider the dress code. If the wedding is a more formal event, it is appropriate to wear a more elaborate outfit with intricate designs and embellishments. For a more casual wedding, a simpler outfit is appropriate.

Personally, I attended a friend's wedding in India and opted for a simple white kurta with a blue Nehru jacket. I paired it with brown leather sandals and minimal accessories. It was a comfortable and stylish outfit that I received many compliments on.

Picking the Right Colors

Colors play a significant role in Indian weddings and are often chosen based on their symbolism. For example, red is a popular color for weddings as it represents love and passion. Other popular colors include gold, green, and orange.

When choosing the colors for your outfit, it is important to consider the bride and groom's color scheme. You don't want to clash with the colors of the wedding or draw attention away from the couple.

Embellishments and Designs

Embellishments and designs are an important aspect of traditional Indian clothing. From intricate embroidery to hand-painted designs, there are many options to choose from.

Personally, I opted for a simple design on my kurta, but I have seen many men wear elaborate embellishments with pride. It ultimately comes down to personal preference and the overall dress code of the wedding.

Caring for Your Traditional Indian Clothing

Proper care for your traditional Indian clothing is essential to maintain its quality and longevity. It is recommended to dry clean your outfit to prevent damage to the delicate fabrics and embellishments.

Additionally, it is important to store your outfit properly to prevent wrinkles or damage. It is recommended to store your outfit in a cool, dry place and avoid folding or hanging it in direct sunlight.

Conclusion of Traditional Indian Clothing for Male Weddings

Attending an Indian wedding is an exciting and significant event, and dressing in traditional attire is an important part of the experience. By following the dress code and choosing the right colors and designs, you can look and feel your best on this special occasion. Remember to properly care for your outfit to ensure its longevity and quality.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a suit to an Indian wedding instead of traditional attire?
A: While it is not recommended to wear a suit to an Indian wedding, it ultimately depends on the dress code of the wedding. It is best to consult with the bride or groom before making a decision.

Q: Do I have to wear a dupatta with my outfit?
A: No, the dupatta is optional and is not required for the traditional attire.

Q: Can I wear traditional Indian clothing to a Western-style wedding?
A: While it is not traditional to wear Indian clothing to a Western-style wedding, it ultimately depends on the dress code and cultural norms of the wedding. It is best to consult with the bride and groom before making a decision.

Q: Can I wear traditional Indian clothing to a non-Indian wedding?
A: While it is not traditional to wear Indian clothing to a non-Indian wedding, it ultimately depends on the dress code and cultural norms of the wedding. It is best to consult with the bride and groom before making a decision.
