Indian Hats: A Beginner's Guide to What They Are Called

Have you ever seen an Indian wearing a hat that looks different from the typical western hats? Have you ever wondered what those hats are called? If you are curious and want to know more about them, then you are in the right place.

The term 'Indian hats' is a general term used to describe different types of headgear worn by people in India. Each state or region has its own unique style of hat, and they are called by different names. While hats in India serve the same purpose as western hats, which is to protect the head from the sun, they also have cultural and religious significance.

In this article, we will explore the different names of Indian hats and their significance in Indian culture and religion.

What are Indian Hats Called?

Indian hats go by different names depending on the region they come from. Here are some of the common Indian hats:

Turban

A turban is a long piece of cloth wrapped around the head. It is commonly worn by Sikh men as a religious requirement. The turban is a symbol of respect, dignity, and self-respect. It is also worn by men in other parts of India for protection from the sun and dust.

Pheta

A Pheta is a type of turban worn by men in Maharashtra. It is usually made of cotton or silk and is worn on special occasions such as weddings or festivals.

Pagri

Pagri is a type of turban worn by men in Punjab. It is usually made of a long cloth wrapped around the head and is worn as a sign of respect and honor.

Safa

Safa is a type of turban worn by men in Rajasthan. It is usually made of cotton or silk and is worn on special occasions such as weddings or festivals.

Topi

A topi is a small hat worn by men in India. It is usually made of cotton and is worn to protect the head from the sun. It is commonly worn by Muslim men.

Phari

A Phari is a type of hat made of bamboo and straw. It is worn by farmers and laborers in rural areas to protect their head from the sun.

These are just a few examples of Indian hats. There are many other types of hats worn in different regions of India.

Significance of Indian Hats

Indian hats have cultural and religious significance in India. Hats like the turban, pagri, and pheta are worn as a sign of respect and honor. They are also considered an important part of traditional attire in India.

Indian hats are also worn as a religious requirement. For example, Sikh men are required to wear a turban as a symbol of their faith. Hats like the topi are also worn by Muslim men as a religious requirement.

Conclusion of What are Indian Hats Called

In conclusion, Indian hats come in various styles and are called by different names depending on the region they are from. Hats in India have cultural and religious significance and are an integral part of traditional attire. By learning about Indian hats and their significance, we can gain a better understanding and appreciation of Indian culture and customs.
