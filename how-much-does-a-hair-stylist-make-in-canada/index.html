How Much Does A Hair Stylist Make In Canada

Written by Frank Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you wondering how much a hair stylist makes in Canada? If you're considering a career as a hair stylist or currently working in the industry, it's essential to know the earning potential in your profession. Knowing how much you can make as a hair stylist in Canada can help you set financial goals and make informed career decisions.

Many factors determine a hair stylist's salary, such as location, experience, and the type of salon they work in. In Canada, hair stylists' salaries range from $25,000 to $60,000 per year, with the average salary being $36,000.

To earn a higher income as a hair stylist, you can specialize in a particular area of hairstyling, such as color or cutting. Additionally, building a loyal clientele and working in a high-end salon can also increase your earning potential.

Personal Experience

As a hair stylist working in Toronto, I have found that my earning potential has increased as I've gained more experience and built a loyal clientele. I specialize in color and have worked in high-end salons, which has helped me earn a higher income than when I started in the industry.

Increasing Your Earnings

There are several ways to increase your earning potential as a hair stylist in Canada. One way is to specialize in a particular area of hairstyling, such as color or cutting. By becoming an expert in a specific area, you can charge higher prices and attract more clients.

Another way to increase your earnings is to build a loyal clientele. By providing excellent service and building relationships with your clients, they are more likely to return and refer others to your salon.

Education and Certification

Education and certification are also essential factors in determining a hair stylist's salary in Canada. Completing a hairstyling program at a recognized institution and obtaining certification can increase your earning potential. Additionally, attending workshops and training sessions to learn new techniques can also help you become more valuable to your clients and employer.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're currently working as a hair stylist and feel that you're not being paid what you're worth, it's essential to negotiate your salary. Before you go into a meeting with your employer, research the average salary for your position in your area and come prepared with examples of your work and accomplishments. Be confident and professional when discussing your salary, and be open to compromise.

Personal Experience

Early in my career as a hair stylist, I felt that I wasn't being paid what I was worth. I did some research and found that the average salary for my position in my area was higher than what I was earning. I scheduled a meeting with my employer, presented my research and examples of my work, and negotiated a higher salary. It was a nerve-wracking experience, but I'm glad I did it as it helped me earn what I deserved.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Hair Stylist Make in Canada

As a hair stylist in Canada, your earning potential can vary depending on several factors, such as location, experience, and specialization.
