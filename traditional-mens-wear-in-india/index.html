Traditional Mens Wear In India A Guide To The Classics

Written by Aprilia Jul 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

India is a country steeped in tradition, and nowhere is this more evident than in its fashion. For men, traditional wear is both practical and stylish, but navigating the many options can be overwhelming. From the vibrant colors to the intricate embroidery, there's a lot to consider. In this guide, we'll break down the basics of traditional mens wear in India and help you make the right choice for any occasion.

When it comes to traditional mens wear in India, there are a few pain points to keep in mind. For one, the sheer variety of options can be overwhelming. From the kurta to the sherwani, each garment has its own unique style and purpose. Additionally, traditional wear can be quite expensive, so it's important to choose wisely.

The target of traditional mens wear in India is to celebrate culture and heritage while also looking stylish and sophisticated. Whether you're attending a wedding or a religious ceremony, traditional wear is a must-have for any Indian man's wardrobe.

To summarize, traditional mens wear in India is a celebration of culture and style. However, with so many options to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. In this guide, we'll explore the different types of traditional mens wear in India and help you make an informed decision.

Kurta: The Everyday Classic

For everyday wear, the kurta is a classic choice. This loose-fitting tunic is made from lightweight cotton or silk and is perfect for staying cool in the hot Indian weather. The kurta comes in a variety of colors and patterns, from simple solids to bold prints. You can dress it up with a pair of churidar pants or keep it casual with a pair of jeans.

Personally, I love wearing a kurta on a lazy Sunday afternoon. It's comfortable, stylish, and perfect for lounging around the house. When I'm running errands or meeting friends for lunch, I'll pair it with a pair of jeans and sandals for a casual look that still looks put-together.

Sherwani: The Formal Favorite

When it comes to formal occasions, the sherwani is the go-to choice for Indian men. This long coat is made from luxurious fabrics like silk and velvet and is often embellished with intricate embroidery and beadwork. The sherwani is typically worn with a pair of churidar pants and a dupatta (a long scarf draped over the shoulders).

My wedding day was one of the most memorable days of my life, and I knew I wanted to wear a sherwani. I opted for a classic cream-colored sherwani with gold embroidery, and I felt like a king. The sherwani is a statement piece that commands attention, and it's perfect for any special occasion.

Dhoti: The Regal Choice

The dhoti is a traditional garment worn by men in India for centuries. This long piece of cloth is draped around the waist and legs, creating a loose and comfortable fit. The dhoti is often worn with a kurta and a vest, making it a great choice for formal events.

While I don't wear a dhoti often, I always feel regal when I do. There's something about the way it flows and drapes that makes me feel like royalty. Plus, it's a great conversation starter!

How to Choose the Right Traditional Mens Wear in India

Choosing the right traditional mens wear in India depends on the occasion and your personal style. For casual events, a kurta is a great choice, while a sherwani is perfect for formal occasions. When in doubt, opt for classic colors like cream, black, or navy blue.

When shopping for traditional wear, keep in mind that quality is key. Look for pieces made from high-quality fabrics like silk or linen, and pay attention to the details like embroidery and beadwork. Additionally, make sure the fit is comfortable and flattering.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a kurta and a kurti?

A: A kurta is a long tunic worn by men, while a kurti is a shorter tunic worn by women.

Q: Can I wear a sherwani to a non-Indian wedding?

A: While a sherwani is traditionally worn to Indian weddings, it can also be worn to other formal events. However, it's important to consider the dress code and cultural context.

Q: How do I accessorize traditional mens wear in India?

A: Accessories like a turban, jootis (traditional Indian shoes), and a dupatta can complete the look. However, it's important not to overdo it â€“ choose one or two accessories that complement the outfit.

Q: Can I wear a kurta with jeans?

A: Yes, a kurta can be paired with jeans for a casual look. Just make sure the fit and colors complement each other.

Conclusion of Traditional Mens Wear in India

Traditional mens wear in India is a celebration of culture and style, and there's a garment for every occasion. From the everyday kurta to the formal sherwani, each piece has its own unique style and purpose. When choosing traditional wear, keep in mind the occasion, your personal style, and the quality of the garment. With the right choice, you'll look and feel your best.
