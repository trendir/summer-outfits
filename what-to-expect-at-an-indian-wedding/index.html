What To Expect At An Indian Wedding A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline May 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their grandeur and extravagance, with a plethora of rituals and ceremonies that can span over several days. Whether you're attending an Indian wedding for the first time or you're a seasoned guest, knowing what to expect can help you navigate through the festivities with ease.

From the attire to the food, there are several pain points that guests may encounter when attending an Indian wedding. For instance, guests may feel overwhelmed by the number of customs and traditions, or they may not know how to dress appropriately for the occasion. However, with a little bit of preparation and understanding, guests can enjoy the festivities without any stress.

If you're wondering what to expect at an Indian wedding, we've got you covered. In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through the various rituals and ceremonies that take place during an Indian wedding, as well as the attire, food, and other important aspects of the celebration.

What to Expect at an Indian Wedding: A Personal Experience

As an Indian-American, attending Indian weddings has been a part of my life since childhood. One of the most memorable weddings I attended was my cousin's, which was a three-day affair that took place in Mumbai, India.

The first day was the Sangeet, which is a pre-wedding celebration where the bride and groom's families come together to sing and dance. The second day was the wedding ceremony itself, which took place at a temple in Mumbai. The third day was the reception, which was a formal affair with a sit-down dinner and dancing.

Attending an Indian wedding can be a truly immersive experience, with vibrant colors, delicious food, and joyful music. However, it's important to understand the various customs and rituals that take place during the celebration.

Understanding Indian Wedding Customs and Traditions

Indian weddings are steeped in tradition, with several customs and rituals that are unique to each region and religion. However, there are some common elements that are present in most Indian weddings.

One of the most important customs is the exchange of garlands between the bride and groom, which signifies their acceptance of each other as partners. Another important ritual is the Seven Steps or Sapthapadi, where the bride and groom take seven vows together, each step symbolizing a different aspect of married life.

Other customs include the Mehendi ceremony, where intricate henna designs are applied on the bride's hands and feet, and the Haldi ceremony, where a paste made of turmeric, sandalwood, and other ingredients is applied on the bride and groom's skin to make them glow.

Indian Wedding Attire

Indian weddings are a visual feast, with guests donning vibrant and colorful traditional attire. Women typically wear sarees or lehengas, which are long skirts paired with a blouse and dupatta (scarf). Men usually wear a sherwani, which is a long coat-like garment paired with trousers.

It's important to dress appropriately for an Indian wedding, and guests should avoid wearing black or white, which are traditionally associated with mourning. Instead, opt for bright and cheerful colors that reflect the joyous occasion.

Indian Wedding Food

Indian cuisine is known for its rich flavors and spices, and guests can expect a veritable feast at an Indian wedding. From savory chaat (snacks) to aromatic biryanis (rice dishes), there's something for everyone.

One of the most popular dishes at Indian weddings is the dessert, which is often a sweet and creamy confection such as gulab jamun or ras malai. Guests should also try the traditional Indian drink, lassi, which is a yogurt-based beverage that comes in sweet and savory flavors.

What to Expect at an Indian Wedding: Final Thoughts

Attending an Indian wedding can be a truly memorable and joyous experience, with a plethora of customs and traditions that reflect the rich cultural heritage of India. By dressing appropriately, understanding the various rituals and ceremonies, and enjoying the delicious food, guests can fully immerse themselves in the festivities.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I wear jeans to an Indian wedding?

A: No, jeans are not appropriate attire for an Indian wedding. Guests should dress formally and wear traditional Indian attire if possible.

Q: Is it appropriate to bring a gift to an Indian wedding?

A: Yes, it is customary to bring a gift for the bride and groom. Money or jewelry are popular gifts, but guests can also give household items or personalized gifts.

Q: Should I participate in the dancing at an Indian wedding?

A: Absolutely! Dancing is a big part of Indian weddings, and guests are encouraged to join in the festivities.

Q: What is the best way to greet the bride and groom at an Indian wedding?

A: Guests can greet the bride and groom by touching their feet as a sign of respect. Alternatively, guests can offer a traditional Indian greeting such as "Namaste" or "Salam."

Conclusion of What to Expect at an Indian Wedding

Attending an Indian wedding can be a truly immersive and joyous experience, with a myriad of customs and rituals that reflect the rich cultural heritage of India. By understanding the various traditions, dressing appropriately, and enjoying the delicious food, guests can fully immerse themselves in the festivities and create lasting memories.
