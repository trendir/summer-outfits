Vintage Clothing Names A Guide To Understanding The Terminology

Written by Oliver Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who loves to shop for vintage clothing but feels overwhelmed by the vast amount of terminology used to describe the garments? It can be difficult to keep up with all the different names and styles, but fear not! In this guide, we will break down some common vintage clothing names and explain their meanings, so you can feel confident when navigating the world of vintage fashion.

When shopping for vintage clothing, it can be frustrating to come across a term you don't understand. This is especially true when shopping online, where you can't physically see the garment and rely solely on the description provided. Understanding vintage clothing names is important because it can help you determine the era the garment is from, its style, and its potential value.

The main target of vintage clothing names is to help identify the specific era and style that the garment represents. This is important because fashion is always evolving, and certain styles are unique to specific time periods. By understanding the terminology, you can better appreciate the history behind the garment and the impact it had on fashion at the time.

In summary, understanding vintage clothing names is crucial for any vintage fashion lover. It can help you determine the era and style of a garment, as well as its potential value. In the following sections, we will break down some common vintage clothing names and explain their meanings in more detail.

Flapper Dresses

Flapper dresses were popular in the 1920s and are characterized by their loose, straight silhouette and drop waist. They were typically made of materials such as silk or chiffon and adorned with fringe or beading. I once found a beautiful beaded flapper dress at a thrift store and felt like I had struck gold. It was the perfect addition to my vintage collection.

Rockabilly

Rockabilly fashion originated in the 1950s and was heavily influenced by rock and roll music. It is characterized by bold prints, high-waisted bottoms, and cropped tops. I remember finding a pair of high-waisted, polka dot shorts at a vintage store and pairing them with a cropped t-shirt. It was the perfect rockabilly-inspired outfit!

Poodle Skirts

Poodle skirts were popular in the 1950s and were typically made of felt. They featured a poodle applique and were often worn with a cinched waist belt. Poodle skirts are a great addition to any vintage collection and are perfect for a retro-themed party or event.

How to Care for Vintage Clothing

When it comes to vintage clothing, proper care is crucial in preserving the garment's integrity. Always check the care label before washing and avoid using harsh chemicals or excessive heat. It's also important to store vintage clothing in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent fading and damage.

Bell Bottoms

Bell bottoms were popular in the 1970s and are characterized by their flared hemline. They were typically made of denim and paired with platform shoes. I once found a pair of vintage bell bottoms at a thrift store and paired them with a colorful blouse for a fun, retro look.

Question and Answer

Q: Are vintage clothing names standardized across different eras?

A: No, vintage clothing names can vary depending on the era and region. It's important to research specific terminology for each era to better understand the garment.


Q: How can I determine the value of a vintage garment?

A: Factors such as the garment's condition, rarity, and designer can all contribute to its value. It's important to do research and consult with experts to determine the garment's worth.


Q: Can vintage clothing be altered to fit my body?

A: Yes, vintage clothing can be altered to fit your body. It's important to consult with a professional tailor who specializes in vintage garments to ensure the integrity of the garment is not compromised.


Q: Where can I find vintage clothing?

A: Vintage clothing can be found at thrift stores, vintage stores, and online marketplaces such as Etsy or eBay.

Conclusion of Vintage Clothing Names

Now that you have a better understanding of some common vintage clothing names, you can feel more confident when shopping for vintage garments. Remember to do your research and consult with experts to ensure you're purchasing authentic vintage pieces. Happy shopping!
