The History of Denim

Denim has been around for centuries, with the first denim fabric being created in the city of Nimes, France, in the 17th century. The fabric was later exported to the United States, where it was used to make workwear for miners and farmers.

In the 1950s, denim became popular with teenagers and young adults, thanks to movies like Rebel Without a Cause and the rise of rock and roll. Jeans became a symbol of rebellion and non-conformity, and the denim industry exploded.
