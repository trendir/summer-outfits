Popular Men s Clothing In The 80S

Written by Petter Sep 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you ever wonder what the fashion scene was like for men in the 80s? Think bright colors, bold patterns, and lots of denim. The 80s was a time of self-expression through fashion, and men's clothing was no exception. Let's take a closer look at the popular men's clothing in the 80s.

The fashion scene in the 80s was all about standing out and making a statement. However, with so many different styles and trends, it could be overwhelming to find the perfect look. Some men may have felt pressure to conform to certain fashion norms, while others may have struggled to find a style that truly represented their personality.

The target of popular men's clothing in the 80s was to create a unique and eye-catching look that would make a statement. Men's clothing styles varied widely, from preppy and polished to edgy and rebellious.

In summary, popular men's clothing in the 80s was all about bold statements and self-expression through fashion. Now, let's dive deeper into some of the most popular styles of the time.

The Preppy Look

The preppy look was one of the most popular styles for men in the 80s. This style was all about clean, polished lines, and conservative colors. Key pieces in the preppy look included collared shirts, khaki pants, and boat shoes. Sweaters were often worn over the shoulders, and blazers were a staple in any preppy wardrobe.

Personally, I remember my father always wearing collared shirts tucked into his khaki pants with a belt. He would finish off the look with a pair of boat shoes or loafers. This style was perfect for the office or a night out with friends.

The Punk Look

The punk look was the complete opposite of the preppy look. This style was all about rebellion and edginess. Key pieces in the punk look included leather jackets, ripped jeans, and band t-shirts. The punk look was often completed with studded belts and combat boots.

I remember my older brother was a big fan of the punk look. He would often wear a leather jacket with a band t-shirt and ripped jeans. He would finish off the look with a pair of combat boots and a studded belt. This style was perfect for a night out at a concert or just hanging out with friends.

The Athletic Look

The athletic look was all about comfort and functionality. This style was perfect for men who were always on the go. Key pieces in the athletic look included sweatpants, track jackets, and sneakers. The athletic look was often completed with a baseball cap or a headband.

Personally, I was a big fan of the athletic look. I would often wear a pair of sweatpants with a track jacket and sneakers. I would finish off the look with a baseball cap or a headband. This style was perfect for a day of running errands or just lounging around the house.

How to Achieve the 80s Look Today

If you're looking to achieve the 80s look today, there are a few key pieces you'll need. For the preppy look, try pairing a collared shirt with khaki pants and boat shoes. For the punk look, try wearing a leather jacket with a band t-shirt and ripped jeans. For the athletic look, try wearing a pair of sweatpants with a track jacket and sneakers.

Remember, the 80s was all about making a statement and expressing yourself through fashion. Don't be afraid to mix and match styles to create a look that truly represents your personality.

The Question and Answer Section

Q: What were some popular colors in men's clothing in the 80s?

A: Popular colors in men's clothing in the 80s included bright neon colors, pastels, and bold primary colors.

Q: What was the most popular type of denim in the 80s?

A: Acid wash denim was the most popular type of denim in the 80s.

Q: What type of shoes were popular in the 80s?

A: Sneakers, boat shoes, and combat boots were all popular types of shoes in the 80s.

Q: What type of accessories were popular in the 80s?

A: Accessories such as studded belts, headbands, and baseball caps were popular in the 80s.

Conclusion of Popular Men's Clothing in the 80s

Popular men's clothing in the 80s was all about self-expression and making a statement through fashion. Whether you were into the preppy look, punk look, or athletic look, there was a style for every personality. Today, you can still achieve the 80s look by incorporating key pieces such as collared shirts, leather jackets, and sweatpants into your wardrobe.
