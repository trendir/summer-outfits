Types of Top Soil

Before you buy top soil, you need to know the different types available. The most common types of top soil are screened top soil, fill dirt, and garden soil.

Screened top soil is the most popular type of top soil. It's a fine, dark soil that has been screened to remove rocks, debris, and other contaminants. Screened top soil is ideal for planting lawns, flowers, and vegetables because it provides good drainage and nutrient retention.

Fill dirt, on the other hand, is a heavier soil that contains more clay and sand. It's often used for filling in low spots, creating berms, and building up areas for landscaping projects.

Garden soil is a type of top soil that has been enriched with organic matter, such as compost, peat moss, and manure. It's designed to provide a nutrient-rich environment for plants to grow.

One way to find a reliable supplier is to ask for recommendations from friends, family, or neighbors who have used top soil before. You can also check online reviews and ratings to find a supplier who has a good reputation in your area.

Delivery and Installation

When buying top soil, you need to consider how you're going to transport and install it. Some suppliers offer delivery services, which can be convenient if you don't have a truck or trailer to transport the top soil yourself. Make sure to ask about the delivery fee and whether the supplier can deliver the top soil to your specific location.

How Much Top Soil Do You Need?

Question and Answer

Q: How much does top soil cost?

A: The cost of top soil can vary depending on the supplier, quality, and quantity. On average, screened top soil can cost between $20 and $50 per cubic yard. Fill dirt is generally cheaper, costing between $5 and $20 per cubic yard.

Q: How do I know if the top soil is good quality?

A: Good quality top soil should be dark and crumbly, with a sweet earthy smell. It should be free of large rocks, debris, and other contaminants. You can also ask the supplier about the top soil's source and whether it's been tested for contaminants.

Q: Can I mix different types of top soil?

A: Yes, you can mix different types of top soil to create a custom blend that meets your specific needs. For example, you can mix screened top soil with garden soil to create a nutrient-rich planting mix.

Q: Can I use top soil for indoor plants?

A: Yes, you can use top soil for indoor plants, but it's important to choose a high-quality, sterile soil mix. Avoid using garden soil or top soil from your yard, as they may contain pests and diseases that can harm your indoor plants.
