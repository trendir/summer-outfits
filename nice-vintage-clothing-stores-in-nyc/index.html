Nice Vintage Clothing Stores In Nyc A Guide To Finding The Best Ones

Written by William Jul 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you on the hunt for some unique and stylish vintage clothing in New York City? Look no further, as we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll be sharing some of the best nice vintage clothing stores in NYC, so you can add some one-of-a-kind pieces to your wardrobe.

When it comes to vintage shopping, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. With so many stores in NYC, it can be hard to know which ones are worth your time and money. Additionally, vintage shopping can be hit or miss - sometimes you'll find something amazing, and other times you'll come up empty-handed. But fear not, as we've done the research for you and found some of the best vintage clothing stores in the city.

Our target for this guide is anyone looking for unique and stylish vintage clothing in NYC. Whether you're a seasoned vintage shopper or new to the game, this guide will have something for you. We'll be sharing some of the best stores in the city, as well as what makes each one special.

So, without further ado, let's dive into our guide to nice vintage clothing stores in NYC.

1. Beacon's Closet

Located in Brooklyn and Manhattan, Beacon's Closet is a staple in the vintage shopping scene in NYC. With a constantly rotating selection of vintage and contemporary clothing, you're sure to find something that catches your eye. What sets Beacon's Closet apart is their commitment to sustainability - they buy, sell, and trade clothing to keep items out of landfills and reduce waste.

Personally, I've found some amazing pieces at Beacon's Closet over the years. From vintage band tees to unique dresses, there's always something interesting to discover.

2. L Train Vintage

Another Brooklyn and Manhattan favorite, L Train Vintage is a must-visit for anyone looking for affordable vintage clothing. With multiple locations, including a massive warehouse in Bushwick, L Train Vintage has a wide variety of items to choose from. They specialize in 90s and early 2000s clothing, so if you're looking for some Y2K fashion inspiration, this is the place to go.

One thing to note about L Train Vintage is that their stores can be a bit overwhelming. With racks upon racks of clothing, it can be hard to know where to start. However, if you're willing to dig, you're sure to find some gems.

3. Awoke Vintage

If you're looking for a more curated vintage shopping experience, check out Awoke Vintage in Williamsburg. With a focus on unique and high-quality vintage pieces, this store feels more like a boutique than a thrift shop. They have a carefully selected collection of clothing, accessories, and home goods, all with a vintage aesthetic.

I've personally found some of my favorite vintage pieces at Awoke Vintage, including a 70s jumpsuit and a 90s slip dress. The prices can be a bit higher than some other vintage stores, but the quality and uniqueness of the items make it worth it.

4. The Vintage Twin

The Vintage Twin is a family-owned vintage shop with locations in Soho and the East Village. What sets this store apart is their commitment to sustainability - they repurpose and upcycle vintage clothing to create one-of-a-kind pieces. They also have a focus on inclusivity, with a wide range of sizes and styles available.

Personally, I love the creativity and uniqueness of The Vintage Twin's pieces. They have everything from reworked denim jackets to vintage band tees, all with a modern twist.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I find designer vintage clothing at these stores?

A: While these stores primarily focus on affordable vintage clothing, you may occasionally come across some designer pieces. Keep in mind that these items may be priced higher than other vintage items.

Q: Are all of these stores affordable?

A: While vintage shopping can be more affordable than buying new clothing, prices can vary depending on the store. L Train Vintage is known for having some of the most affordable prices, while Awoke Vintage and The Vintage Twin may be a bit pricier.

Q: Do these stores only sell women's clothing?

A: While some vintage stores may have a larger selection of women's clothing, all of the stores on this list have options for all genders.

Q: What should I bring with me when vintage shopping?

A: It's always a good idea to wear comfortable clothing and shoes, as vintage shopping can involve a lot of walking and trying on clothes. Additionally, bring a reusable shopping bag to carry your purchases in, as some vintage stores may not provide bags.

Conclusion of Nice Vintage Clothing Stores in NYC

Whether you're a vintage shopping pro or new to the game, there's something special about finding a unique and stylish piece of clothing from a bygone era. By checking out some of the best vintage clothing stores in NYC, you're sure to find something that speaks to your personal style. From Beacon's Closet to The Vintage Twin, these stores offer a wide variety of vintage clothing options, all with their own unique flair.
