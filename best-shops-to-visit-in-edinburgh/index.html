Best Shops To Visit In Edinburgh A Guide To Shopping In The Scottish Capital

Written by Bardi Jul 04, 2023 · 5 min read

Edinburgh is known for its stunning architecture, rich history, and vibrant culture, but it's also a great destination for shopping. Whether you're looking for high-end fashion or unique, locally-made souvenirs, the city has something for everyone. In this guide, we'll highlight the best shops to visit in Edinburgh and help you make the most of your shopping experience.

If you're a tourist visiting Edinburgh, you might be overwhelmed with the number of shops and stores available in the city. You might not know where to start or which shops are worth your time and money. Additionally, if you're not familiar with the local culture, you might miss out on hidden gems that only locals know about. That's where this guide comes in - we'll help you navigate the city's shopping scene and find the best shops to suit your needs and interests.

Best Shops to Visit in Edinburgh: High-End Fashion and Luxury Goods

If you're looking for high-end fashion and luxury goods, Edinburgh has plenty of options for you. One of the most iconic shopping destinations in the city is Multrees Walk, a street lined with designer boutiques and luxury stores. You'll find brands like Louis Vuitton, Harvey Nichols, and Burberry here, as well as high-end Scottish brands like Johnstons of Elgin and Hamilton & Inches.

If you're looking for something a bit more unique, visit the Grassmarket area in the Old Town. This historic area is full of independent shops and boutiques, offering everything from vintage clothing to handmade jewelry. You'll also find a variety of second-hand bookstores and record shops here, making it a great destination for book and music lovers.

Best Shops to Visit in Edinburgh: Local Souvenirs and Handmade Goods

If you're looking for local souvenirs and handmade goods, Edinburgh has plenty of options for you as well. One of the most popular destinations for souvenir shopping is the Royal Mile, a street that runs through the heart of the Old Town. Here you'll find shops selling everything from tartan scarves and kilts to Scottish whisky and shortbread.

Another great destination for handmade goods is the West End of the city. This area is home to a variety of independent shops and galleries, offering everything from handmade ceramics to locally-made skincare products. You'll also find a variety of vintage and retro clothing stores here, making it a great destination for fashion lovers as well.

Best Shops to Visit in Edinburgh: Food and Drink

No shopping experience in Edinburgh is complete without trying some of the city's famous food and drink. One of the best destinations for foodies is the Stockbridge neighborhood, located in the New Town area. Here you'll find a variety of specialty food stores and artisanal bakeries, as well as a weekly farmers' market selling fresh, local produce.

If you're a fan of whisky, make sure to stop by the Scotch Whisky Experience on the Royal Mile. Here you can learn about the history and production of Scotland's national drink, as well as taste a variety of whiskies from different regions of the country.

Best Shops to Visit in Edinburgh: Shopping Tips and Tricks

Before you start your shopping adventure in Edinburgh, here are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind:

  • Wear comfortable shoes - Edinburgh is a city of hills and cobblestone streets, so you'll want to be prepared for lots of walking.
  • Bring cash - while most shops and stores in Edinburgh accept credit cards, some smaller or independent shops might only take cash.
  • Visit during the off-season - Edinburgh can get very crowded during peak tourist season, so consider visiting in the shoulder season to avoid crowds and get better deals.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the best time of year to go shopping in Edinburgh?

A: The best time of year to go shopping in Edinburgh depends on your preferences. If you don't mind crowds and want to experience the city at its liveliest, consider visiting during the summer months (June-August). However, if you want to avoid crowds and get better deals, consider visiting in the shoulder season (April-May or September-October).

Q: Are there any local Scottish brands that I should look out for while shopping in Edinburgh?

A: Yes! Edinburgh is home to a variety of Scottish brands that you won't find anywhere else. Some of our favorites include Johnstons of Elgin (known for their cashmere products), Mackintosh (known for their raincoats), and Harris Tweed (known for their tweed fabrics).

Q: Can I get VAT (Value Added Tax) refunds when shopping in Edinburgh?

A: Yes. If you're a non-EU resident and spend more than £30 in a single store, you're eligible for a VAT refund. Make sure to ask for a VAT refund form when you make your purchase, and present it at the airport when you leave the country.

Q: Can I negotiate prices in Edinburgh shops?

A: Generally, no. While negotiating prices might be common in some other parts of the world, it's not typically expected or accepted in Edinburgh shops. However, if you're buying multiple items or making a large purchase, it never hurts to ask if there are any discounts available.

Conclusion of Best Shops to Visit in Edinburgh

Whether you're a fashion lover, a foodie, or a souvenir collector, Edinburgh has something for everyone when it comes to shopping. From high-end boutiques to local markets, the city offers a diverse range of shopping experiences that are sure to satisfy any traveler. By following the tips and recommendations in this guide, you'll be able to make the most of your shopping adventure in the Scottish capital.
