Discover The Best Places To Buy Vintage Clothes

Written by Eveline Jul 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of shopping at the same stores and seeing everyone else wearing the same clothes as you? Do you want to stand out with unique and stylish outfits? Vintage clothing may be the answer you're looking for. However, finding the best places to buy vintage clothes can be a challenge. You may not know where to start your search or be afraid of getting ripped off. In this post, we'll explore the best places to buy vintage clothes and give you tips on how to make the most of your vintage shopping experience.

If you're looking for one-of-a-kind pieces that are sustainable and environmentally friendly, vintage clothing is an excellent choice. Vintage clothing is unique, high-quality, and often more affordable than buying new clothes. However, vintage shopping can be daunting, especially if you're new to it. You may not know where to find the best vintage shops or how to spot a valuable vintage piece. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we'll share our favorite places to buy vintage clothes and give you tips on how to navigate the vintage shopping world.

When it comes to vintage shopping, there are a few different options. You can shop online, visit vintage shops, or attend flea markets or thrift stores. Each option has its pros and cons, depending on your preferences and budget. We've rounded up the best places to buy vintage clothes based on our personal experiences and recommendations from vintage enthusiasts.

In summary, the best places to buy vintage clothes include online vintage shops, local vintage shops, flea markets, and thrift stores. Each option has its advantages and disadvantages, so it's essential to decide which one works best for you. When shopping for vintage clothes, it's important to do your research and know what you're looking for. With these tips and recommendations, you're sure to find some unique and stylish vintage pieces to add to your wardrobe.

Online Vintage Shops

Online vintage shops are a great way to shop for vintage clothes from the comfort of your own home. These shops offer a wide selection of vintage clothing, from designer labels to everyday wear. Some of our favorite online vintage shops include Etsy, Beyond Retro, and ASOS Marketplace.

Personally, I love shopping at Etsy for vintage clothes. It's a treasure trove of unique and stylish vintage pieces, and you can find anything from vintage band tees to vintage designer dresses. The prices are reasonable, and you can often find a great deal. However, it's essential to read the seller's reviews and check the measurements before making a purchase. Another great online vintage shop is Beyond Retro. They have a vast selection of vintage clothing, and you can filter your search by decade or style. The prices are on the higher side, but the quality is excellent. Finally, ASOS Marketplace is a great option if you're looking for vintage designer pieces. They have a curated selection of vintage clothing from top designers, but the prices can be steep.

Local Vintage Shops

Local vintage shops are an excellent option if you want to try on clothes and see them in person before making a purchase. These shops often have a unique selection of vintage clothing that you can't find anywhere else. Plus, you're supporting a small business in your community. Some of our favorite local vintage shops include Rokit, The Stellar Boutique, and Beyond Retro (they also have physical stores).

Personally, I love shopping at Rokit for vintage clothes. They have a great selection of affordable vintage pieces, from 80s windbreakers to 70s bohemian dresses. The quality is excellent, and the staff is knowledgeable about vintage clothing. Another great local vintage shop is The Stellar Boutique. They have a unique selection of vintage and eco-friendly clothing, including upcycled pieces. The prices are on the higher side, but the quality is exceptional. Finally, Beyond Retro is a great option if you're looking for a mix of vintage and new clothing. They have physical stores in London and Sweden, as well as an online shop.

Flea Markets

Flea markets are a fun and exciting way to shop for vintage clothes. You never know what you'll find, and the prices are often negotiable. Flea markets are also an excellent way to support local vendors and find unique pieces that you can't find anywhere else. Some of our favorite flea markets for vintage shopping include Brooklyn Flea, Rose Bowl Flea Market, and Portobello Road Market.

Personally, I love shopping at the Brooklyn Flea for vintage clothes. It's a great way to spend a Saturday afternoon, browsing through vintage clothing, furniture, and other unique finds. The prices are reasonable, and you can often find a great deal. Another great flea market for vintage shopping is the Rose Bowl Flea Market in Pasadena, California. It's one of the largest flea markets in the world and has a vast selection of vintage clothing and accessories. Finally, Portobello Road Market in London is a great option if you're looking for vintage clothing and antiques. It's a bustling market with over 1,000 vendors, so be prepared to spend a few hours browsing.

Thrift Stores

Thrift stores are an excellent option if you're on a budget and looking for affordable vintage clothes. These stores often have a mix of vintage and modern clothing, but you can find some real gems if you're willing to search. Some of our favorite thrift stores for vintage shopping include Goodwill, Salvation Army, and Buffalo Exchange.

Personally, I love shopping at Goodwill for vintage clothes. It's a great way to support a good cause while finding some unique vintage pieces. You have to be willing to search, but you can find some real gems for a fraction of the price of new clothes. Another great thrift store for vintage shopping is Salvation Army. They often have a mix of vintage and modern clothing, but you can find some great vintage pieces if you're willing to look. Finally, Buffalo Exchange is a great option if you're looking for trendy vintage pieces. They have a curated selection of vintage and modern clothing, and the prices are reasonable.

Question and Answer

Q: What should I look for when shopping for vintage clothes?

A: When shopping for vintage clothes, it's essential to look for quality and condition. Look for items that are well-made and in good condition. Check for any stains, tears, or holes. It's also important to check the sizing, as vintage clothing often runs smaller than modern sizes.

Q: How can I tell if a vintage piece is valuable?

A: There are a few things to look for when determining the value of a vintage piece. Look for designer labels, unique prints or patterns, and high-quality materials. Research the era the item is from and compare it to similar items from that time period. It's also essential to consider the condition of the item and its rarity.

Q: How can I style vintage pieces?

A: Vintage pieces can be styled in a variety of ways, depending on the item and your personal style. Try pairing a vintage blouse with high-waisted jeans and ankle boots for a classic look. Or, layer a vintage dress over a turtleneck and tights for a trendy outfit. Don't be afraid to mix vintage pieces with modern items for a unique and stylish look.

Q: How can I care for vintage clothing?

A: Vintage clothing requires special care to preserve its condition. Always read the care tag and follow the instructions carefully. Hand wash or dry clean vintage clothing, as machine washing can damage the fabric. Store vintage clothing in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.

Conclusion of Best Places to Buy Vintage Clothes

Vintage clothing is a unique and sustainable way to update your wardrobe. Whether you're shopping online, at a local vintage shop, flea market, or thrift store, there are plenty of options to choose from. When shopping for vintage clothes, it's essential to do your research, know what you're looking for, and be patient. With these tips and recommendations, you're sure to find some stylish and unique vintage pieces to add to your wardrobe.
