What Color Should You Not Wear To An Indian Wedding

Written by Luffy Jun 05, 2023 · 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors, rich traditions, and festive atmosphere. However, there are certain customs and beliefs that need to be respected when attending an Indian wedding, including the choice of clothing. This can be especially challenging for non-Indian guests who may not be familiar with the customs and traditions. In this blog, we will discuss what color you should not wear to an Indian wedding to avoid any cultural faux pas.

Attending an Indian wedding can be an exciting and memorable experience, but it can also be overwhelming, especially when it comes to choosing the right outfit. Wearing the wrong color can be considered disrespectful and offensive to the couple and their families, and it can also attract unwanted attention. Therefore, it is important to understand the significance of colors in Indian culture and avoid any colors that are considered inappropriate.

The color that you should not wear to an Indian wedding is black. In Indian culture, black is traditionally associated with mourning and sadness, and it is considered inauspicious for joyous occasions like weddings. It is believed that wearing black can bring bad luck and negative energy to the newlyweds, which is why it is best to avoid this color altogether.

In summary, if you are attending an Indian wedding, it is important to be mindful of the cultural norms and traditions. Avoid wearing black as it is considered inappropriate and disrespectful. Instead, opt for bright and cheerful colors like red, pink, orange, yellow, and green, which are considered auspicious and bring good luck and positive energy.

Why is Black Considered Inappropriate for Indian Weddings?

When I attended my first Indian wedding, I was unsure about what to wear, and I was surprised to learn that black was not an option. I later learned that black is traditionally associated with mourning and sadness in Indian culture, and it is considered inauspicious for joyous occasions like weddings. It is believed that wearing black can bring bad luck and negative energy to the newlyweds, which is why it is best to avoid this color altogether.

Are There Any Other Colors That Should Be Avoided?

While black is the color that should be avoided at all costs, there are a few other colors that should be approached with caution. White is another color that is traditionally associated with mourning in Indian culture, and it is best to avoid it unless it is a part of the bride's outfit. Similarly, red and green should be avoided if the bride is wearing these colors as they are considered to be auspicious for the bride only.

What Colors Are Considered Auspicious for Indian Weddings?

In Indian culture, bright and cheerful colors are considered auspicious for weddings as they symbolize happiness, prosperity, and good luck. Some of the popular colors for Indian weddings include red, pink, orange, yellow, and green. However, it is important to note that the significance of colors can vary depending on the region and community.

How Can You Incorporate Indian Elements into Your Outfit?

If you want to incorporate Indian elements into your outfit, you can opt for traditional Indian attire like a saree, lehenga, or salwar kameez. These outfits are available in a wide range of colors and styles, and they can be accessorized with jewelry, bangles, and a bindi. Alternatively, you can also wear a fusion outfit that combines Indian and Western styles, like a crop top with a skirt or palazzo pants.

My Personal Experience

When I attended my friend's Indian wedding, I was amazed by the vibrant colors and intricate designs of the outfits. I decided to wear a bright pink saree with gold jewelry and a matching bindi. I received many compliments on my outfit, and I felt like I had truly embraced the spirit of the occasion. It was a memorable experience, and I learned a lot about Indian culture and traditions.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear black to an Indian reception or engagement ceremony?

A: It is best to avoid wearing black to any event related to the wedding as it is considered inauspicious and disrespectful.

Q: Can I wear a white saree to an Indian wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing white to an Indian wedding as it is traditionally associated with mourning and sadness.

Q: Can I wear a red dress to an Indian wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing red to an Indian wedding if the bride is wearing the same color as it is considered to be auspicious for the bride only.

Q: Can I wear jeans to an Indian wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing jeans to an Indian wedding as it is considered too casual and disrespectful.

Conclusion

In conclusion, if you are attending an Indian wedding, it is important to be respectful of the cultural norms and traditions. Black is the color that should be avoided at all costs as it is considered inauspicious and disrespectful. Instead, opt for bright and cheerful colors that symbolize happiness, prosperity, and good luck.
