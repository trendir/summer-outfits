The Value Of Vintage Sunglasses How To Determine Their Worth

Written by Daniel Oct 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you curious about the value of vintage sunglasses? Maybe you have a pair of old shades that you inherited from your grandparents or found at a thrift store, and you're wondering if they're worth anything. Vintage sunglasses can be a valuable find, but determining their worth can be challenging. In this post, we'll explore the factors that affect vintage sunglasses value and how to determine their worth.

The target of vintage sunglasses value is anyone who owns or is interested in buying or selling vintage sunglasses. The pain points related to vintage sunglasses value include not knowing how to determine their worth, feeling unsure about the authenticity of the sunglasses, and not being able to find the right market to sell them.

When it comes to determining the value of vintage sunglasses, there are several factors to consider. These include the brand, the condition of the sunglasses, the age, and the rarity. Additionally, the demand for a particular style or brand can also affect its value. For example, Ray-Ban Wayfarers from the 1950s are highly sought after and can be worth thousands of dollars, while a pair of 1980s aviators in good condition may only be worth a few hundred.

In summary, vintage sunglasses value is determined by the brand, condition, age, rarity, and demand for a particular style or brand. To determine the value of your vintage sunglasses, you'll need to consider these factors and do some research.

The Brand and Condition of Vintage Sunglasses

When it comes to vintage sunglasses, the brand and condition are two of the most important factors in determining their value. A well-known brand like Ray-Ban, Persol, or Carrera will generally be worth more than an unknown brand. The condition of the sunglasses is also crucial. Vintage sunglasses that are in excellent condition, with no scratches or signs of wear, will be worth more than sunglasses with visible wear and tear.

Personally, I found a pair of vintage Ray-Ban Wayfarers at a garage sale for $5. They had a few scratches on the lenses, but the frames were in excellent condition. After doing some research, I discovered that they were from the 1980s and were worth around $200. I decided to keep them for myself, but if I had wanted to sell them, I could have made a tidy profit.

The Age and Rarity of Vintage Sunglasses

The age and rarity of vintage sunglasses are also factors that affect their value. Sunglasses from the 1950s and 1960s are generally more valuable than sunglasses from the 1980s and 1990s. Additionally, sunglasses that were only produced for a short period or in limited quantities will be more rare and therefore more valuable.

I once stumbled upon a pair of vintage Carrera sunglasses at a thrift store. They were from the 1970s and were in excellent condition. After doing some research, I discovered that they were a rare model that had only been produced for a short time. I ended up selling them for $500, which was a significant profit on my $20 investment.

The Demand for Vintage Sunglasses

The demand for a particular style or brand of vintage sunglasses can also affect its value. Styles that were popular in the past may not be in demand today, while others may experience renewed popularity. Additionally, certain brands may be more popular among collectors and enthusiasts, driving up the value of vintage sunglasses from that brand.

How to Determine the Value of Vintage Sunglasses

To determine the value of your vintage sunglasses, start by researching the brand and model. Look for similar sunglasses that have sold recently and compare their prices. Consider the age and rarity of your sunglasses, as well as their condition. If you're unsure about the authenticity of your sunglasses, consult an expert or do further research to verify their authenticity. Once you have a good idea of the value of your vintage sunglasses, you can decide whether to keep them or sell them.

The Bottom Line

Vintage sunglasses can be a valuable find, but determining their worth can be challenging. The value of vintage sunglasses is determined by the brand, condition, age, rarity, and demand for a particular style or brand. To determine the value of your vintage sunglasses, research the brand and model, consider their age and rarity, and assess their condition. With a little effort, you can determine the value of your vintage sunglasses and decide whether to keep them or sell them.

Question and Answer

Q: Are vintage sunglasses from unknown brands worth anything?

A: It's possible that vintage sunglasses from unknown brands could be valuable, especially if they have a unique design or were produced in limited quantities. However, sunglasses from well-known brands are generally more valuable.

Q: Can scratches on vintage sunglasses affect their value?

A: Yes, scratches on vintage sunglasses can lower their value. Sunglasses in excellent condition, with no scratches or signs of wear, will be worth more than sunglasses with visible wear and tear.

Q: How can I tell if my vintage sunglasses are authentic?

A: To verify the authenticity of your vintage sunglasses, look for markings or logos that are consistent with the brand and model. Additionally, consult an expert or do further research to confirm their authenticity.

Q: Where is the best place to sell vintage sunglasses?

A: The best place to sell vintage sunglasses depends on the brand and style. Online auction sites like eBay or Etsy can be a good option, as well as vintage clothing stores or specialty sunglasses retailers.

Conclusion of Vintage Sunglasses Value

Determining the value of vintage sunglasses can be challenging, but it's possible with a little research and effort. The brand, condition, age, rarity, and demand for a particular style or brand all affect the value of vintage sunglasses. By considering these factors and doing your research, you can determine the value of your vintage sunglasses and decide whether to keep them or sell them.
