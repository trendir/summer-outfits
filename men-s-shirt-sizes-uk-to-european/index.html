Even if you know your size in one country, it can be completely different in another. Understanding Men's Shirt Sizes UK to European

When it comes to men's shirt sizes, there are two main sizing systems: UK and European. UK sizing is based on neck size in inches, while European sizing is based on chest size in centimeters. This means that if you know your neck size in inches, you can easily convert it to a UK size. However, if you know your chest size in centimeters, you'll need to convert it to a European size.

Personally, I've found that European sizing tends to be more accurate for me. I've had instances where a UK size that should fit me doesn't, whereas a European size that should be too small ends up fitting perfectly. It's important to keep in mind that sizing can vary between brands, so it's always a good idea to check the sizing chart before making a purchase.

Tips for Finding the Perfect Fit

While knowing your size is important, there are other factors to consider when it comes to finding the perfect fit. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Check the sizing chart for each brand you're interested in, as sizing can vary between brands.
  • Consider the fit you prefer. Do you like a slim fit or a more relaxed fit?
  • Measure yourself to ensure you're getting the most accurate size.
  • Try the shirt on before purchasing if possible.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a sales associate.

FAQs

What is the difference between UK and European shirt sizes?

UK shirt sizes are based on neck size in inches, while European shirt sizes are based on chest size in centimeters. This means that a UK size 16 would have a neck size of 16 inches, while a European size 41 would have a chest size of 41cm.

How do I convert UK shirt sizes to European?

To convert UK shirt sizes to European, you can use the conversion chart above. Simply find your UK neck size and look for the corresponding European chest size.

Is European sizing more accurate than UK sizing?

It depends on the brand and the individual. Some people may find that UK sizing is more accurate for them, while others may find that European sizing is more accurate. It's important to check the sizing chart for each brand and to measure yourself to ensure you're getting the most accurate size.

What should I do if I'm between sizes?

If you're between sizes, it's generally recommended to size up rather than down. It's easier to take in a shirt that's too big than to try to stretch out a shirt that's too small.
