What Styles Were Popular In The 80S

Written by Frank Oct 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you ever wonder what it was like to live in the 80s? The decade was full of bright neon colors, funky hairstyles, and bold fashion choices. It was a time of self-expression and individuality, where people let their creativity shine through their clothing choices. In this blog post, we'll explore what styles were popular in the 80s and how they continue to influence fashion today.

The 80s was an era of excess, and the fashion reflected that. With the rise of MTV and music videos, fashion became a way to express oneself and stand out from the crowd. However, there were still some pain points related to fashion in the 80s. For example, some people felt pressure to conform to the popular trends, while others felt excluded from certain fashion choices due to their gender or socioeconomic status.

The 80s was a time of experimentation and self-expression, and this was reflected in the fashion choices of the decade. Some of the most popular styles included oversized blazers, acid-wash jeans, neon colors, and bold prints. Accessories like jelly bracelets, scrunchies, and leg warmers were also popular during this time.

In summary, the 80s was a time of excess and self-expression, where fashion was used as a way to stand out and make a statement. Popular styles included oversized blazers, acid-wash jeans, neon colors, and bold prints. Accessories like jelly bracelets, scrunchies, and leg warmers were also must-haves during this era.

The Power of Shoulder Pads

One of the most iconic styles from the 80s was the shoulder pad trend. Shoulder pads were often found in blazers, dresses, and even t-shirts. They were used to create a more structured and powerful look, and they quickly became a staple in women's fashion during the decade.

Personally, I remember wearing shoulder pads to school dances and feeling like a total boss. They made me feel confident and empowered, and I loved how they added a unique touch to my outfits.

Shoulder pads continue to influence fashion today, with many designers incorporating them into their collections. Whether you prefer a subtle shoulder pad or a more exaggerated version, this trend is a timeless classic that will always be associated with the 80s.

The Rise of Hip Hop Fashion

The 80s was also a time of significant change in the music industry, particularly with the rise of hip hop. Hip hop fashion quickly became a trend, with baggy jeans, sneakers, and tracksuits becoming popular among young people.

As a fan of hip hop music, I loved incorporating this style into my wardrobe. I remember wearing baggy jeans and oversized t-shirts, and feeling like I was part of a cultural movement.

Today, hip hop fashion continues to be a significant influence on streetwear and urban fashion. Many designers take inspiration from hip hop culture, incorporating elements of the trend into their collections.

Neon Colors and Bold Prints

Neon colors and bold prints were also popular during the 80s. These styles were often seen in clothing and accessories, and they were used to create bold and eye-catching looks.

Personally, I loved wearing neon colors and bold prints. They made me stand out and express my creativity, and I always enjoyed experimenting with different combinations.

Today, neon colors and bold prints continue to be popular, particularly during the summer months. Many designers incorporate these trends into their collections, creating unique and vibrant pieces.

Accessorizing with Scrunchies

Scrunchies were a staple accessory during the 80s, and they continue to be popular today. These hair ties were often made with bright colors and bold prints, and they were used to add a pop of color to any outfit.

Personally, I loved wearing scrunchies in my hair and on my wrist. They were a fun and playful accessory that added a unique touch to my outfits.

Today, scrunchies continue to be a popular accessory, with many designers creating unique and stylish versions of the classic hair tie.

Question and Answer

Q: What were some popular 80s fashion trends for men?

A: Some popular 80s fashion trends for men included acid-wash jeans, leather jackets, and bomber jackets. Accessories like aviator sunglasses and snapback hats were also popular during this time.

Q: What was the punk fashion trend in the 80s?

A: The punk fashion trend in the 80s was characterized by ripped clothing, leather jackets, and studded accessories. This style was often associated with rebellion and anti-establishment values.

Q: What were some popular shoes in the 80s?

A: Some popular shoes in the 80s included high-top sneakers, cowboy boots, and penny loafers. Athletic shoes like Nike Air Jordans and Reebok Freestyles were also popular during this time.

Q: How did 80s fashion influence fashion today?

A: 80s fashion continues to influence fashion today, particularly in the areas of streetwear and athleisure. Many designers take inspiration from 80s fashion trends, incorporating elements like neon colors, bold prints, and oversized silhouettes into their collections.

Conclusion of What Styles Were Popular in the 80s

In conclusion, the 80s was a decade of excess and self-expression, where fashion was used as a way to stand out and make a statement. Popular styles included oversized blazers, acid-wash jeans, neon colors, and bold prints. Accessories like jelly bracelets, scrunchies, and leg warmers were also must-haves during this era. While some of these trends may seem outdated today, they continue to influence fashion and inspire designers around the world.
