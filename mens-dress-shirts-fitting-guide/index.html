How to find the perfect fit for your men's dress shirt

Buying the perfect dress shirt can be challenging. You might find a shirt that looks good on the hanger but doesn't quite fit right when you put it on. Or maybe you've struggled to find a shirt that fits comfortably around the neck without feeling too tight or too loose. These common issues can make shopping for men's dress shirts a frustrating experience.

The ultimate goal of this guide is to help you find the perfect fitting dress shirt. We'll cover everything from measuring your size to understanding the different types of fits available.

In summary, this guide will cover:

  • How to measure your size for a dress shirt
  • The different types of dress shirt fits available
  • Tips for finding the perfect fit
  • Common fit issues and how to fix them

Measuring for Your Dress Shirt

Before you start shopping for dress shirts, it's important to measure yourself to determine your size. Most dress shirts are sized based on neck and sleeve measurements.

To measure your neck, use a soft measuring tape and measure around the base of your neck, just below your Adam's apple. For sleeve length, measure from the center of your back, along your shoulder, down to your wrist.

Once you have your measurements, you can use a size chart to determine your dress shirt size. Keep in mind that different brands may have slightly different sizing, so it's always a good idea to check the size chart before making a purchase.

The Different Types of Dress Shirt Fits

There are several different types of dress shirt fits available, each with its own unique style and fit. Here are some of the most common:

  • Classic Fit: This fit is relaxed and comfortable, with extra room in the chest and waist.
  • Regular Fit: This fit is slightly more fitted than a classic fit, with a closer cut through the chest and waist.
  • Slim Fit: This fit is tapered through the chest and waist, with a closer fit overall.
  • Extra Slim Fit: This fit is even more tapered than a slim fit, with a very close fit through the chest and waist.

It's important to try on different fits to determine which one works best for you. Keep in mind that different brands may have slightly different fits, so it's always a good idea to try on different brands and styles.

Tips for Finding the Perfect Fit

When shopping for a dress shirt, it's important to keep a few things in mind to ensure the perfect fit:

  • Pay attention to the fit in the shoulders. The shoulder seam should sit right at the edge of your shoulder.
  • Make sure the collar fits comfortably around your neck, with enough room for you to slide two fingers in between the collar and your neck.
  • The sleeves should be long enough to cover your wrist bone, but not so long that they bunch up.
  • Choose a fit that flatters your body shape. If you're on the slimmer side, a slim fit or extra slim fit may work best. If you have a broader build, a classic fit or regular fit may be more comfortable.

Common Fit Issues and How to Fix Them

Even with careful measuring and trying on different fits, you may still encounter some fit issues. Here are some common fit issues and how to fix them:

  • If the shirt is too loose in the chest or waist, consider trying a slimmer fit or having the shirt tailored to fit better.
  • If the shirt is too tight in the chest or waist, try a looser fit or consider having the shirt tailored to fit better.
  • If the sleeves are too long, have them shortened by a tailor.
  • If the sleeves are too short, try a different size or brand that offers longer sleeves.
  • If the collar is too tight or too loose, try a different size or brand that offers a better fit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I measure myself at home for my dress shirt?

A: Yes, you can measure yourself at home using a soft measuring tape.

Q: How do I know if the shirt fits well?

A: The shirt should fit comfortably in the shoulders, chest, and waist, with sleeves that are long enough to cover your wrist bone. The collar should also fit comfortably around your neck.

Q: Can I have a dress shirt tailored to fit better?

A: Yes, having a dress shirt tailored is a great way to ensure a perfect fit.

Q: How do I choose the right fit for my body shape?

A: If you're on the slimmer side, a slim fit or extra slim fit may work best. If you have a broader build, a classic fit or regular fit may be more comfortable.

Conclusion

Finding the perfect fitting dress shirt doesn't have to be a challenge. By measuring yourself, trying on different fits, and following our tips, you can find a shirt that looks great and feels comfortable. Remember that different brands may have slightly different sizing and fits, so it's always a good idea to try on different brands and styles to find the perfect fit for you.
