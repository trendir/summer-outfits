The Best Italian Cake Recipes You Need To Try

Written by Frank Jun 08, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of the same old desserts? Do you want to impress your guests with something new and delicious? Look no further than the best Italian cake recipes. These recipes offer a unique twist on classic desserts and are sure to have everyone asking for seconds.

When it comes to baking, Italian desserts are a crowd favorite. From rich chocolate cakes to light and fluffy sponge cakes, there is a recipe for everyone. But with so many options, it can be overwhelming to choose which recipe to try. That's where we come in.

In this article, we will introduce you to the best Italian cake recipes and provide you with everything you need to know to make them yourself. From ingredients to step-by-step instructions, we've got you covered.

So, let's dive in and discover the world of Italian desserts.

Tiramisu Cake

One of the most popular Italian desserts is tiramisu. This coffee-flavored dessert is made with ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, and cocoa powder. But have you ever tried tiramisu cake? This cake takes all the flavors of tiramisu and turns it into a beautiful and decadent dessert.

My personal experience with tiramisu cake was when I first tasted it at a friend's party. I was blown away by the combination of coffee and mascarpone cheese. It was creamy, rich, and everything you could want in a dessert. I immediately asked for the recipe and have been making it for special occasions ever since.

To make tiramisu cake, you will need:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup strong coffee
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Follow these steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add in the eggs, one at a time, then add in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually mix in the flour mixture, alternating with the milk until the batter is smooth.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pans and bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cakes to cool completely before assembling the cake.
  8. Combine the coffee and mascarpone cheese in a medium bowl, mixing until smooth.
  9. In a separate large bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  10. Gently fold the mascarpone mixture into the whipped cream until fully combined.
  11. Place one cake layer on a plate and spread ⅓ of the mascarpone mixture on top. Repeat with the second layer and remaining mascarpone mixture.
  12. Dust the top of the cake with cocoa powder and chill for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Cannoli Cake

Another beloved Italian dessert is cannoli. These crispy pastry shells are filled with sweet ricotta cheese and chocolate chips. But have you ever tried cannoli cake? This cake takes all the flavors of a traditional cannoli and turns it into a show-stopping dessert.

I first tried cannoli cake at a local Italian bakery and was blown away by the combination of flavors and textures. The moist cake paired perfectly with the creamy ricotta filling and crunchy cannoli shells. I knew I had to try making it myself.

To make cannoli cake, you will need:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 6-8 cannoli shells, crushed

Follow these steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add in the eggs, one at a time, then add in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually mix in the flour mixture, alternating with the milk until the batter is smooth.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cake to cool completely before assembling the cake.
  8. In a large bowl, beat the ricotta cheese until smooth.
  9. In a separate large bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and cinnamon until stiff peaks form.
  10. Gently fold the ricotta cheese into the whipped cream until fully combined.
  11. Spread the ricotta filling on top of the cooled cake.
  12. Sprinkle the mini chocolate chips and crushed cannoli shells on top of the ricotta filling.
  13. Chill the cake for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Biscotti Cake

When most people think of biscotti, they think of a dry, crunchy cookie that is perfect for dipping in coffee or tea. But have you ever tried biscotti cake? This cake takes all the flavors of biscotti and turns it into a moist and delicious dessert.

I first tried biscotti cake at an Italian restaurant and was pleasantly surprised by how moist and flavorful it was. The combination of almond and orange flavors was perfect, and I knew I had to try making it myself.

To make biscotti cake, you will need:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract
  • 2 tablespoons orange zest
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 1 cup chopped biscotti

Follow these steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add in the eggs, one at a time, then add in the vanilla extract, almond extract, and orange zest.
  5. Gradually mix in the flour mixture, alternating with the milk until the batter is smooth.
  6. Fold in the chopped biscotti.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cake to cool completely before serving.

Conclusion of Best Italian Cake Recipes

Italian desserts are some of the most delicious and unique desserts out there. From tiramisu cake to cannoli cake to biscotti cake, there is a recipe for everyone. These cakes take classic Italian flavors and turn them into beautiful and decadent desserts.

Next time you want to impress your guests or try something new, try making one of these best Italian cake recipes. You won't be disappointed.
